<div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><p><strong>Windows 11:</strong></p><p>1) Click on the folder on the bottom bar</p><p>2) Click <strong>This PC</strong></p><p>3) Click on the <strong>Computer</strong> tab</p><p>4) Click <strong>Map Network Drive</strong></p><p>5) Enter the path to your department share. This may be on Dss10 or fas-depts</p><p>For example, <a href="https:////fas-depts.ad.fas.harvard.edu/cgis/your_department_name">\\fas-depts.ad.fas.harvard.edu\cgis\your_department_name</a> or \\dss10.ad.fas.harvard.edu\cgis\your_department</p><p>6) Check both boxes that say<strong> Reconnect at login</strong> and <strong>Connect using different credentials</strong></p><p>7)<strong> </strong>Click <strong>Finish</strong>, and when prompted enter your username as shown below:</p><p><strong>FAS_Domain\your_username</strong></p><p>And your <strong>Harvard Key password</strong></p><p> </p><p><strong>Mac:</strong></p><p>1) In the Finder, Click the <strong>Go</strong> menu, and select <strong>connect to server</strong></p><p>2) Enter smb://fas-depts.ad.fas.harvard.edu/cgis/your_department or smb://dss10.ad.fas.harvard.edu/cgis/your_department</p><p>3) When asked, enter you Harvard username and Harvard Key password</p><p> </p><p>Please note:</p><p>If you are off site or using the Harvard Wireless networks, you will need to use Cisco VPN to connect to vpn.fas.harvard.edu before attempting to map a shared drive. For assistance with using the VPN please <a href="mailto:help@iq.harvard.edu">contact us</a>.</p></div>
