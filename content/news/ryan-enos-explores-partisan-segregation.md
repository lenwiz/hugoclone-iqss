Professor of Government Ryan Enos has spent much of his career exploring how local geographic context shapes political behavior and partisan segregation. His research reveals that where people live—and who their neighbors are—profoundly influences their political attitudes and behaviors.

Enos's work demonstrates that partisan segregation is self-reinforcing: as communities become more politically homogeneous, residents develop more extreme views and lose the ability to understand opposing perspectives. This finding has important implications for democratic governance and social cohesion.

As an IQSS affiliate, Enos brings sophisticated spatial analysis methods to questions about political geography, combining GIS data, survey research, and field experiments to build a comprehensive picture of how physical space shapes political life in America.
