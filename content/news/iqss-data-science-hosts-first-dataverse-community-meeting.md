IQSS Data Science Services hosted the inaugural Dataverse Community Meeting, bringing together repository administrators, developers, and data professionals from institutions worldwide for the first gathering centered on the Dataverse platform. This historic event marked an important milestone in the evolution of Dataverse from a Harvard project into a global open-source community, creating opportunities for knowledge sharing, collaborative problem-solving, and collective planning for the platform's future development.

The first Dataverse Community Meeting featured presentations about repository implementations, technical workshops, discussions of data curation challenges, and planning sessions for future software development. Attendees shared experiences managing Dataverse repositories in diverse institutional contexts, from small university libraries to large international collaborations. The gathering enabled face-to-face connections among people who had primarily interacted through online channels, strengthening relationships and building the sense of community essential for successful open-source projects.

This inaugural meeting established what has become an essential annual tradition for the Dataverse ecosystem. The success of the first gathering demonstrated strong interest in a community-driven approach to developing and sustaining research data repository infrastructure. The meeting model—combining technical content, case studies, and community building—has continued through subsequent years as the Dataverse community has grown. The 2015 event in Cambridge laid the foundation for an international network of institutions committed to collaborative development of open-source tools for research data management and sharing.
