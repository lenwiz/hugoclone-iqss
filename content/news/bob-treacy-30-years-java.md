Bob Treacy, Principal Software Architect on the Dataverse Project at IQSS, recently shared his remarkable journey from working on factory floors to becoming a key architect of one of the world's leading open-source research data repository platforms. His story offers unique insights into the evolution of software development over three decades and the enduring relevance of Java, the programming language that has been central to his career and to the Dataverse platform.

Treacy's path into software development was unconventional, shaped by hands-on problem-solving experience in industrial settings before transitioning into professional programming. He witnessed Java's emergence in the mid-1990s and has stayed with the language through its evolution from a novel web-oriented platform to the enterprise workhorse it is today. His perspective on Java's 30-year history provides valuable context for understanding why Dataverse continues to be built on this mature, stable technology despite the constant emergence of new programming languages and frameworks.

In discussing the role of artificial intelligence in research software development, Treacy offers a balanced view of how AI tools are beginning to assist developers while emphasizing the continued importance of human expertise, architectural thinking, and deep understanding of research workflows. His insights reflect the experience of someone who has navigated multiple waves of technological change and understands both the potential and limitations of new tools in the complex domain of research data management software.
