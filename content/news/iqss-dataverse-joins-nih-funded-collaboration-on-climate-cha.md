IQSS's Dataverse Project has joined an NIH-funded collaboration focused on climate change and health, bringing research data repository expertise to a major interdisciplinary initiative examining the health impacts of environmental change. This partnership reflects growing recognition that climate change poses profound threats to human health through pathways including extreme heat, air quality degradation, infectious disease patterns, food security, and mental health impacts. Understanding and addressing these challenges requires integration of climate science, epidemiology, and health research—and the data infrastructure to support such work.

Dataverse's role in this collaboration involves providing repository infrastructure for datasets generated through climate-health research projects, ensuring that valuable data about climate impacts on health outcomes is preserved, accessible, and reusable by the scientific community. The platform's capabilities for managing diverse data types, supporting controlled access where necessary to protect privacy, and enabling data citation align well with the needs of this interdisciplinary research domain. The partnership also facilitates development of best practices and metadata standards specific to climate-health data.

This collaboration demonstrates Dataverse's expanding role beyond traditional social science data into interdisciplinary research domains where data sharing infrastructure is essential for scientific progress. Climate change and health research generates complex datasets requiring sophisticated repository capabilities, and Dataverse's participation in this NIH-funded initiative helps ensure that researchers have access to appropriate data management solutions. The work contributes to the broader goal of accelerating scientific understanding of climate impacts on human health and supporting evidence-based responses to this growing threat.
