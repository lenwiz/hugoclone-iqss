The OpenDP Project is thrilled to announce the first in-person OpenDP Community Meeting, bringing together researchers, developers, and practitioners working at the intersection of data privacy, statistical analysis, and open-source software development. This historic gathering marks an important milestone for the OpenDP ecosystem, providing a venue for face-to-face collaboration, knowledge sharing, and community building around practical differential privacy tools.

The meeting will feature presentations on the latest developments in the OpenDP Library, case studies from organizations deploying differential privacy in production systems, and technical workshops that help participants gain hands-on experience with privacy-preserving data analysis. Sessions will cover topics ranging from fundamental concepts for newcomers to advanced techniques for experienced practitioners, ensuring valuable content for attendees at all levels. The gathering also provides crucial opportunities for informal discussions, networking, and collaborative problem-solving that are difficult to replicate in virtual settings.

This first in-person meeting represents the maturation of the OpenDP community from an open-source software project into a vibrant ecosystem of practitioners advancing the state of practical privacy protection. Researchers, data scientists, engineers, policy makers, and anyone interested in responsible data use are invited to participate. The event will help shape the future direction of OpenDP development and strengthen connections among individuals and institutions working to make differential privacy accessible and practical for protecting sensitive data across all sectors.
