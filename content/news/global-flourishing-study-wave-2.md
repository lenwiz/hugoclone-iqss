The Human Flourishing Program at Harvard University has released Wave 2 data from the Global Flourishing Study, marking a significant milestone in one of the most comprehensive longitudinal research efforts on human well-being ever undertaken. This new wave of data is now freely available to researchers worldwide through the Center for Open Science, continuing the study's commitment to open science and collaborative research.

The Wave 2 extended datasets build upon the groundbreaking first wave by tracking the same cohort of approximately 200,000 participants across 22 countries over time. This longitudinal design allows researchers to examine how flourishing changes across different life stages and in response to various life events and circumstances. The data captures information across six key domains of human flourishing: happiness and life satisfaction, physical and mental health, meaning and purpose, character and virtue, close social relationships, and financial and material stability.

The release of this second wave data provides unprecedented opportunities for researchers to study trends in human well-being across diverse cultural, geographic, and economic contexts. The Global Flourishing Study, led by principal investigators Tyler VanderWeele at Harvard and Byron Johnson at Baylor University in collaboration with Gallup, will continue tracking participants over a five-year period, creating an invaluable resource for understanding what contributes to human flourishing worldwide.
