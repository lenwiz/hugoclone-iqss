In an episode of Harvard Thinking, IQSS affiliate Michael Brenner explores one of the most pressing questions facing educators today: how has artificial intelligence affected learning and critical thinking, and how can these powerful tools be embraced to enhance rather than diminish educational outcomes? Brenner, the Glover Professor of Applied Mathematics and Applied Physics at Harvard, brings both technical expertise and pedagogical insight to this critical discussion.

Brenner examines the double-edged nature of AI in educational contexts. While AI tools can provide instant answers and automate routine tasks, there is growing concern that students may become overly reliant on these technologies, bypassing the cognitive struggle that leads to deep understanding and skill development. The conversation addresses how educators can design learning experiences that leverage AI's capabilities while ensuring students still develop fundamental thinking skills, creativity, and problem-solving abilities that remain uniquely human.

Rather than viewing AI as a threat to education, Brenner advocates for a thoughtful integration approach that uses artificial intelligence as a complement to human intelligence. He discusses strategies for helping students understand when and how to use AI tools effectively, while maintaining the kind of rigorous intellectual engagement that builds lasting knowledge and capability. The discussion offers valuable insights for educators, students, and anyone concerned with preserving meaningful learning in an era of increasingly powerful AI assistants.
