The China Biographical Database (CBDB) aims at identifying and cataloging individuals across millennia of Chinese society and their shared connections. This ambitious digital humanities project, based at IQSS, represents one of the largest biographical databases ever constructed for historical research.

CBDB contains biographical information from Chinese historical records spanning the 7th through 19th centuries, enabling researchers to conduct statistical analysis, social network analysis, and spatial analysis of historical Chinese society. The database reveals patterns of social mobility, political networks, and cultural transmission that traditional historical methods could not detect.

By making this data freely accessible, CBDB enables scholars worldwide to study Chinese history using quantitative methods, bridging the gap between digital humanities and traditional Sinological scholarship. The project continues to grow as researchers contribute new data from previously untapped historical sources.
