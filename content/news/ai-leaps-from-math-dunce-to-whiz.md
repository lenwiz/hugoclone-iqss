IQSS affiliate Michael Brenner, the Michael F. Cronin Professor of Applied Mathematics and Applied Physics, explains how recent AI advancements have opened new possibilities for mathematical application. What was once considered a fundamental limitation of artificial intelligence—its inability to perform complex mathematical reasoning—has been dramatically overcome by newer model architectures.

Brenner discusses how these advances enable researchers to tackle problems that were previously computationally intractable, from solving differential equations to discovering new mathematical theorems. The implications extend across scientific disciplines where mathematical modeling plays a central role.

The rapid improvement in AI's mathematical capabilities has particular relevance for social science research, where complex statistical methods and large-scale data analysis are increasingly essential. IQSS researchers are well-positioned to leverage these tools in their work across economics, political science, sociology, and related fields.
