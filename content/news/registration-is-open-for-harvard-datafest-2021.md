Harvard's Institute for Quantitative Social Science is excited to announce that registration is now open for Harvard DataFest 2021, an intensive bootcamp designed to help researchers develop, refine, and advance their research projects through hands-on learning and collaborative problem-solving. This immersive event brings together graduate students, postdoctoral researchers, and faculty members working across the social sciences to tackle methodological challenges and accelerate their scholarly work.

DataFest provides participants with concentrated time to work on their research while accessing expert guidance on quantitative methods, computational tools, and data management strategies. The bootcamp format combines structured workshops on specific technical topics with dedicated project time where attendees can apply new skills to their own research questions. Experienced instructors and peer participants create a collaborative learning environment where researchers can troubleshoot challenges, discover new approaches, and make meaningful progress on projects that might otherwise stall due to technical obstacles.

The event is particularly valuable for researchers who need focused time away from regular responsibilities to push projects forward, learn new methodological techniques, or overcome specific analytical hurdles. Whether you're working with survey data, administrative records, textual corpora, or experimental datasets, DataFest offers tools and expertise to enhance your research. The 2021 virtual format ensures accessibility for researchers regardless of location while maintaining the intensive, productive atmosphere that makes DataFest such a valuable experience. Registration details and program information are available on the IQSS website.
