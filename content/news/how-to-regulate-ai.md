IQSS affiliate David Y. Yang, Professor of Economics at Harvard University, has joined other leading experts in addressing one of the most pressing policy questions of our time: how to effectively regulate artificial intelligence development and deployment. As US legislators pass measures at both state and federal levels, the debate over AI regulation has intensified, with scholars and policymakers grappling with balancing innovation against risks ranging from privacy violations and algorithmic bias to potential existential threats from advanced AI systems.

Yang's expertise in applied economics, political economy, and empirical methods brings valuable perspective to discussions about AI governance. The challenge of regulating AI involves navigating fundamental tensions between promoting technological progress and protecting public interests, determining appropriate roles for government versus industry self-regulation, and creating frameworks that can keep pace with rapidly evolving capabilities. Different jurisdictions are taking varied approaches, from comprehensive regulatory frameworks to more targeted interventions focused on specific applications or sectors.

The conversation among experts including Yang explores questions about what aspects of AI development and deployment most urgently require regulation, how to design rules that are effective without stifling beneficial innovation, and whether international coordination is necessary given AI's global nature. These discussions contribute to shaping policy approaches that will profoundly influence how AI technologies develop and integrate into society over the coming decades, with implications for economic competitiveness, civil liberties, safety, and the distribution of AI's benefits and risks across society.
