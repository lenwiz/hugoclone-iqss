Seth Soderborg explores how partisan politics in Indonesia can affect pandemic preparedness decisions, drawing parallels with U.S. experiences during COVID-19. His research examines whether the politicization of public health measures observed in American politics also manifests in other democratic contexts.

The study finds that in Indonesia, the relationship between partisanship and pandemic preparedness depends significantly on local context—particularly the political alignment between local and national leaders. This geographic variation provides leverage for understanding how political incentives can either promote or undermine public health responses.

Soderborg's work contributes to a growing body of research on the political determinants of health outcomes, demonstrating that the intersection of politics and public health is not uniquely American but a feature of democratic systems more broadly.
