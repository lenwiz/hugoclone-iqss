The Human Flourishing Program, IQSS's newest scientific program, combines social science and philosophy to understand the factors that lead to a good life. Directed by Tyler VanderWeele, the program integrates knowledge from empirical research and the humanities on topics including religion, virtue, work, purpose, forgiveness, marriage, and family.

The program's interdisciplinary approach sets it apart from traditional academic research centers. By bringing together researchers from psychology, epidemiology, philosophy, and theology, the Human Flourishing Program examines well-being through multiple lenses simultaneously, producing insights that no single discipline could achieve alone.

Since its founding at IQSS in 2016, the program has grown into a leading center for data-driven research on flourishing, launching major initiatives including the Global Flourishing Study—one of the largest longitudinal studies of well-being ever conducted, spanning 22 countries and nearly 200,000 participants.
