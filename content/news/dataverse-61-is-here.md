The Dataverse Project is pleased to announce the release of version 6.1, the first feature release following the major 6.0 platform upgrade. This release showcases the strength of community-driven development, with significant contributions from institutions and developers across the global Dataverse network who are collectively shaping the future of open-source research data infrastructure.

Version 6.1 builds on the solid foundation established in 6.0, introducing new features and enhancements that improve functionality for researchers and repository administrators. Community contributions in this release span a wide range of improvements, from user interface refinements and workflow optimizations to new API capabilities and administrative tools. These collaborative efforts demonstrate how the open-source model enables institutions to pool expertise and resources, creating solutions that benefit the entire ecosystem rather than individual repositories in isolation.

The release also includes important bug fixes and stability improvements identified during the 6.0 adoption period. Repository administrators who have successfully upgraded to 6.0 are encouraged to move to 6.1 to benefit from these enhancements and fixes. The strong community engagement reflected in this release underscores Dataverse's position as the leading collaborative platform for research data management, trusted by hundreds of institutions worldwide to support open science, enable reproducible research, and preserve valuable scholarly data for future generations.
