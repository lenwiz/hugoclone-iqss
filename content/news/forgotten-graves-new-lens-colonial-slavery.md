Harvard archaeologist Jason Ur pivoted during the pandemic to studying Cambridges Old Burying Ground, a colonial-era cemetery with ties to slavery. Using drones, ground-penetrating radar, and geospatial databases, Ur mapped the cemetery and discovered spatial patterns of racial segregation in death. Ground-penetrating radar revealed approximately 50 unmarked burials near the graves of enslaved people.
