The 2023 OpenDP Community Meeting was held on September 29th at Harvard University, bringing together researchers, practitioners, and developers focused on differential privacy. The event was organized in conjunction with the TPDP (Theory and Practice of Differential Privacy) workshop, marking the first in-person gathering for the OpenDP community since the project's inception.

Attendees explored the latest developments in the OpenDP Library, discussed real-world deployment challenges, and shared insights on building privacy-preserving data analysis systems. Presentations covered topics ranging from new algorithmic implementations to user experience improvements in the DP Creator application.

The meeting reinforced OpenDP's commitment to building trustworthy, open-source tools for differential privacy that can serve both academic researchers and organizations handling sensitive data. Community feedback gathered during the event will help shape the project's roadmap for future releases.
