IQSS faculty affiliate Cynthia Dwork, Gordon McKay Professor of Computer Science, received the 2020 Donald E. Knuth Prize from SIGACT for her impactful work in the foundations of computer science. The prize recognizes sustained excellence in theoretical computer science research over an entire career.

Dwork is best known as the inventor of differential privacy, a mathematical framework that enables statistical analysis of databases while provably protecting individual privacy. This work has had enormous practical impact, with differential privacy now deployed by the U.S. Census Bureau, Apple, Google, and other major organizations.

Her contributions extend beyond privacy to include foundational work on distributed computing, cryptography, and algorithmic fairness. At IQSS, Dwork's research on privacy and fairness directly connects to the institute's mission of enabling data-intensive social science while protecting individual rights.
