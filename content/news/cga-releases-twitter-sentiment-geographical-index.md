The Center for Geographic Analysis has released the Twitter Sentiment Geographical Index, an innovative tool that derives well-being indicators from social media data to track emotional patterns and public sentiment across geographic regions. This project demonstrates how large-scale analysis of publicly available social media posts can complement traditional surveys and administrative data to provide more timely, granular insights into population well-being and how it varies across space.

The Twitter Sentiment Geographical Index employs natural language processing and sentiment analysis techniques to categorize the emotional content of tweets, then aggregates these sentiment scores at various geographic scales from neighborhoods to cities to regions. By analyzing millions of social media posts over time, researchers can identify patterns in population mood, track responses to events or policies, and detect geographic variations in subjective well-being that might not be captured by conventional data sources. The tool provides a near-real-time window into collective emotional states across communities.

While social media data has important limitations—Twitter users are not demographically representative of the general population, and sentiment analysis algorithms face challenges interpreting meaning from brief text messages—the index provides valuable complementary information to traditional well-being measures. The methodology has applications across research domains including public health surveillance, urban planning, political science, and disaster response. The release of this tool reflects CGA's mission to advance geographic analysis methods and make spatial data resources available to the research community for studying important social phenomena.
