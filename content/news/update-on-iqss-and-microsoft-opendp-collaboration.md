IQSS and Microsoft provided an update on their collaboration to develop open-source tools for differential privacy, enabling researchers to discover insights while protecting data. The partnership combines Harvard's theoretical expertise with Microsoft's engineering resources.

The update details progress on the OpenDP platform, which provides verified privacy guarantees for data analysis. The collaboration has produced both a software library (the OpenDP Library) and research advancing the theoretical foundations of privacy-preserving computation.

This ongoing partnership represents a model for how academic institutions and technology companies can work together on problems of shared interest. For IQSS, it advances the mission of enabling data-intensive social science; for Microsoft, it supports the development of privacy technologies that can be deployed across their products and services.
