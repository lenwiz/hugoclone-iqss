Tyler VanderWeele, the John L. Loeb and Frances Lehman Loeb Professor of Epidemiology at the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health and director of the Human Flourishing Program at IQSS, has been appointed by Pope Leo XIV to the Pontifical Academy of Social Sciences. This prestigious appointment recognizes VanderWeele's groundbreaking work on human flourishing, well-being, and the role of faith and virtue in health and society.

The Pontifical Academy of Social Sciences serves as an advisory body to the Vatican on matters related to social, economic, and political issues affecting humanity. VanderWeele joins an international group of distinguished scholars who provide guidance to the Holy See on complex social questions. His expertise in epidemiological methods, causal inference, and the measurement of well-being brings a rigorous quantitative social science perspective to the academy's deliberations on human development and flourishing.

VanderWeele's appointment reflects the growing recognition of his Human Flourishing Program's contributions to understanding what constitutes a life well-lived across diverse cultural and religious contexts. His research has explored connections between religious practice, meaning and purpose, social relationships, and various health outcomes, work that aligns closely with the Vatican's interests in promoting human dignity and integral human development. This appointment positions VanderWeele to contribute his research insights to discussions of global social challenges at the highest levels of the Catholic Church.
