DataFest 2020 brought together researchers from across Harvard and beyond for an intensive two-day series of workshops and presentations focused on analyzing, visualizing, and managing data in the modern research environment. This annual event, organized by IQSS, provides graduate students, postdoctoral researchers, and faculty with concentrated exposure to cutting-edge data science methods, tools, and best practices that enhance their research capabilities.

The 2020 program featured sessions covering the full lifecycle of data-intensive research, from data collection and cleaning through exploratory analysis, statistical modeling, visualization, and reproducible research workflows. Expert instructors demonstrated contemporary tools and techniques including programming in R and Python, version control with Git, effective data visualization principles, and approaches to managing complex datasets. The intensive format allowed participants to gain hands-on experience with new methods while connecting with peers facing similar methodological challenges across diverse research domains.

DataFest exemplifies IQSS's commitment to building research capacity across Harvard's social science community by providing accessible, high-quality training in quantitative methods and data science. The gathering creates a collaborative learning environment where researchers can ask questions, troubleshoot challenges, and discover new approaches that advance their work. For many participants, DataFest serves as a catalyst for adopting new computational methods and connecting with the broader community of data-intensive researchers at Harvard. The event has become a valued annual tradition that strengthens the methodological sophistication of quantitative research across the university.
