IQSS affiliate Daniel Gilbert, the Edgar Pierce Professor of Psychology at Harvard University, delivered a compelling lecture exploring the intersection of scientific research and timeless maternal wisdom about happiness. Gilbert, known for his groundbreaking work on affective forecasting and happiness research, shared insights from decades of psychological studies that validate many of the common-sense principles our mothers have been telling us for years.

During the lecture, Gilbert explained how rigorous scientific investigation into human happiness has repeatedly confirmed conventional wisdom about what makes people truly happy. From the importance of gratitude and social connections to the value of helping others and maintaining perspective during difficult times, research data consistently supports the age-old advice that mothers have been dispensing for generations. Gilbert's presentation highlighted how psychological science has progressed from questioning these intuitive beliefs to understanding the mechanisms that make them effective.

The lecture resonated with audiences by bridging the gap between academic research and everyday life, demonstrating that sometimes the simplest advice, grounded in human experience and passed down through generations, contains profound truths that modern science is only now beginning to fully understand and appreciate.
