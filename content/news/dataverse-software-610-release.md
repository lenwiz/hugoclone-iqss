The Dataverse Project has announced the release of version 6.10 of the Dataverse software, bringing significant improvements to the world's most widely adopted open-source research data repository platform. This latest release includes enhanced export capabilities, new application programming interfaces (APIs), infrastructure and performance updates, and numerous bug fixes that improve stability and user experience for repositories worldwide.

The export improvements in version 6.10 make it easier for researchers to extract their data and metadata in various formats, supporting interoperability with other research tools and platforms. New APIs expand the programmatic capabilities available to developers and repository administrators, enabling more sophisticated integrations and automation workflows. Infrastructure and performance enhancements ensure that Dataverse continues to scale effectively as datasets grow larger and repository usage increases.

The 6.10 release reflects the collaborative nature of the Dataverse open-source community, with contributions from developers at institutions around the world. Repository administrators are encouraged to review the release notes and plan their upgrades to take advantage of these improvements. The Dataverse team continues its commitment to regular releases that enhance functionality while maintaining the stability and reliability that has made Dataverse the trusted choice for research data management at hundreds of institutions globally.
