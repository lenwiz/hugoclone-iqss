The OpenDP Project is seeking data analysts, researchers, and data professionals to participate in a software usability study focused on DP Creator, an innovative application designed to make differential privacy more accessible to practitioners without deep technical expertise in privacy mathematics. This research study aims to gather valuable feedback that will shape the development of tools that enable broader adoption of privacy-preserving data analysis methods.

DP Creator represents an important step toward democratizing differential privacy by providing intuitive interfaces for creating differentially private analyses without requiring users to understand the underlying mathematical complexities. The software study will involve testing the application with realistic data analysis tasks, providing feedback on the user interface and workflow, and sharing insights about how differential privacy tools can better serve the needs of working data analysts. Participants' experiences and suggestions will directly inform ongoing development to make the tool more effective and user-friendly.

This call for participants reflects OpenDP's commitment to building practical, usable tools that bring the benefits of differential privacy to a wide range of organizations and use cases. Data analysts from academic research, government agencies, healthcare organizations, and private sector companies are all encouraged to consider participating. The study provides an opportunity to contribute to the future of privacy-preserving data analysis while gaining early exposure to emerging technologies that may become increasingly important in data practice. Interested individuals can find more information about participation requirements and how to volunteer on the OpenDP website.
