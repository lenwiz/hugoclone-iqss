Jennifer Lerner, IQSS affiliate and Bradshaw Professor of Public Policy at Harvard Kennedy School, examines how emotion and gender factor into decision making. Her research challenges long-held assumptions about rationality and shows that emotions can both help and hinder good judgment depending on the context.

Lerner's work has revealed systematic patterns in how specific emotions influence economic decisions, risk assessment, and policy preferences. For example, anger tends to make people more optimistic about risks and more punitive in their judgments, while sadness promotes more careful deliberation but can also lead to impatience in financial decisions.

Her research has important implications for public policy design, organizational management, and individual well-being. By understanding the precise mechanisms through which emotions shape decisions, policymakers and leaders can create environments that promote better outcomes for everyone.
