IQSS Director Gary King and Nate Persily of Stanford Law School developed a new model for academic collaboration with industry, addressing the growing tension between researchers' need for data and companies' obligations to protect user privacy. Their working paper proposes frameworks for structured data access.

The model acknowledges that most data about people is now held by private companies rather than governments or academic institutions, creating a fundamental challenge for social science research. King and Persily argue that incentive-compatible arrangements can benefit both parties—companies gain research insights while academics access data that would otherwise be unavailable.

This work directly informs IQSS's broader strategy of building bridges between academia and industry, recognizing that the future of social science depends on developing new institutional arrangements for data access that respect privacy while enabling discovery.
