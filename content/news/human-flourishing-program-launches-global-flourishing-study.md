The Human Flourishing Program at Harvard's IQSS has launched the ambitious Global Flourishing Study, a groundbreaking international research initiative designed to explore fundamental questions about what it means to live well across diverse cultures and societies. This comprehensive, multi-year study represents one of the most extensive efforts ever undertaken to understand human well-being in all its dimensions, moving beyond narrow measures of happiness or economic prosperity to examine flourishing holistically.

The Global Flourishing Study surveys tens of thousands of participants across more than 20 countries spanning all populated continents, measuring flourishing across multiple domains including physical and mental health, happiness and life satisfaction, meaning and purpose, character and virtue, close social relationships, and financial stability. By collecting longitudinal data over several years, the study will track how flourishing evolves over time and identify factors that support or undermine human well-being in different contexts. The research brings together expertise from multiple disciplines including psychology, sociology, economics, public health, and philosophy.

Led by Professor Tyler VanderWeele and conducted in collaboration with leading research institutions including Baylor University's Institute for Studies of Religion and Gallup, the study aims to provide evidence that can inform policies, practices, and interventions to promote human flourishing worldwide. The initiative is supported by major philanthropic foundations committed to understanding and advancing human well-being. By making data openly available to researchers globally, the Global Flourishing Study will enable countless additional investigations into the nature of the good life.
