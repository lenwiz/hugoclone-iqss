IQSS is pleased to announce new space arrangements in the CGIS Knafel Building designed to better serve the research community as we adapt to post-pandemic work patterns and embrace more flexible approaches to office space utilization. These changes reflect lessons learned during the pandemic about how researchers work most effectively and acknowledge the evolving needs of our community as members balance in-person collaboration with remote work flexibility.

The new arrangements include a hybrid approach to office space that accommodates both dedicated workspace for staff and programs while providing bookable desk space for affiliated researchers who need on-campus work areas on a flexible basis. This system enables more efficient use of IQSS facilities while ensuring that community members have access to professional workspace when they need it. The changes also support collaboration by creating more shared spaces where researchers can connect with colleagues, access specialized resources, and participate in the intellectual life of the institute.

IQSS community members are encouraged to familiarize themselves with the new space booking system and policies for accessing shared work areas. The administrative team is available to answer questions and help affiliates navigate the new arrangements. These changes represent IQSS's commitment to creating a physical environment that supports excellent research while adapting to the realities of how scholarly work happens in the contemporary academic environment. The institute looks forward to welcoming researchers back to CGIS and continuing to build the vibrant intellectual community that has always been central to IQSS's mission.
