The OpenDP team announced the release of OpenDP Library 0.8, a modular collection of algorithms for building privacy-preserving applications. This version focuses on expanding the library's capabilities while maintaining its commitment to mathematical rigor and verified privacy guarantees.

Version 0.8 introduces new transformations and measurements, improved documentation, and enhanced interoperability with common data science workflows. The release reflects ongoing collaboration between Harvard's OpenDP team and the broader differential privacy research community.

The OpenDP Library is available on PyPI for Python users and as a Rust crate for systems programmers. It provides building blocks that developers can compose to create custom privacy-preserving analyses, ensuring that the resulting computations provably satisfy differential privacy definitions. The library continues to serve as a key component of IQSS's privacy research infrastructure.
