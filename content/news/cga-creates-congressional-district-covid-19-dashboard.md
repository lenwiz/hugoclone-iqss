The Center for Geographic Analysis has created a comprehensive public dashboard that tracks COVID-19 data by congressional district, providing policymakers, researchers, and the public with geographically granular information about the pandemic's spread and impact across the United States. This innovative tool fills a critical gap in COVID-19 data visualization by organizing information at the congressional district level, enabling more effective policy responses and helping lawmakers understand how the pandemic affects their constituents.

The Congressional District COVID-19 Dashboard aggregates case counts, hospitalizations, testing rates, and other key pandemic indicators, presenting them in an accessible, interactive format that allows users to explore patterns across districts and compare trends over time. By aligning health data with political geography, the dashboard supports evidence-based policy discussions and helps identify districts facing particular challenges that may require targeted interventions or resource allocation. The tool demonstrates how geographic information systems can be rapidly deployed to address urgent public health needs.

The dashboard exemplifies CGA's capacity to respond quickly to emerging societal challenges by combining technical expertise in spatial data analysis with understanding of policy-relevant questions. The project required integrating data from multiple sources, developing appropriate geographic aggregation methods, and creating visualizations that communicate complex information clearly to diverse audiences. The Congressional District COVID-19 Dashboard has served as a valuable resource for journalists, researchers, public health officials, and legislators seeking to understand and communicate about the pandemic's geographic dimensions.
