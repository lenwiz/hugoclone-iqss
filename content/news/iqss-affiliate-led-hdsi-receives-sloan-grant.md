The Harvard Data Science Initiative (HDSI), led by IQSS affiliate Professor Xiao-Li Meng, has received a prestigious $1 million grant from the Alfred P. Sloan Foundation to advance research at the intersection of causal inference and machine learning. This significant funding recognizes the importance of integrating these two powerful approaches to data analysis and supports HDSI's mission to advance data science methods that address pressing societal challenges.

The grant will support research that bridges causal inference—the rigorous statistical framework for understanding cause-and-effect relationships—with machine learning techniques that excel at finding patterns in complex, high-dimensional data. This integration is crucial for many applications where researchers need not just to predict outcomes but to understand what interventions will actually cause desired changes. The work has important implications across fields including public health, education policy, economics, and social science research.

Under Professor Meng's leadership and with support from IQSS's broader ecosystem of quantitative methodologists, HDSI will convene researchers, develop new methods, and train the next generation of data scientists who can work at this important methodological frontier. The Sloan Foundation grant enables Harvard to maintain its position at the forefront of data science innovation while ensuring that methodological advances translate into practical tools that benefit researchers and policymakers working on real-world problems.
