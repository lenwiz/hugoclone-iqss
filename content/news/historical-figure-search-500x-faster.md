The China Biographical Database (CBDB) team at IQSS redesigned their name indexing method to make text analysis research considerably faster—achieving a 500-fold improvement in search performance. The optimization enables researchers to search across thousands of years of Chinese historical records with unprecedented speed.

The breakthrough came from rethinking how the database indexes Chinese names, which present unique computational challenges due to the nature of Chinese characters and historical naming conventions. The previous approach required sequential scanning of large text corpora, but the new indexing method allows near-instantaneous lookup even across millions of historical records.

This performance improvement has dramatic practical implications for scholars working with CBDB. Research projects that previously required days of computational time can now be completed in minutes, enabling more ambitious analyses of social networks, political structures, and cultural patterns across Chinese history. The optimization also makes the database more accessible to researchers who may not have access to high-performance computing resources, democratizing access to this invaluable historical resource.
