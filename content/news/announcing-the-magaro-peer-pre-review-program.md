IQSS is proud to announce the launch of the Alexander and Diviya Magaro Peer Pre-Review Program, an innovative initiative designed to improve scholarly research quality and accelerate publication success by providing authors with rigorous peer feedback before journal submission. This program addresses a common challenge in academic publishing: manuscripts often receive first peer feedback many months after journal submission, leading to lengthy revision cycles that slow the advancement of knowledge.

The Peer Pre-Review Program provides authors with expert, field-specific reviews of their manuscripts in a much shorter timeframe than traditional journal peer review. By receiving constructive feedback before submission, researchers can address methodological issues, strengthen arguments, and improve presentation while their work is still in development. This early intervention saves time in the publication process and improves the ultimate quality of published research. The program particularly benefits early-career researchers who may have limited access to senior colleagues who can provide detailed manuscript feedback.

Made possible through the generous support of Alexander and Diviya Magaro, the program reflects IQSS's commitment to supporting high-quality social science research throughout the entire scholarly lifecycle. The initiative assembles a network of experienced reviewers across social science disciplines who provide the same rigorous, constructive peer review that journals require, but at a stage where feedback can have maximum impact. Eligible researchers are encouraged to learn more about submission procedures and take advantage of this valuable resource to strengthen their scholarship.
