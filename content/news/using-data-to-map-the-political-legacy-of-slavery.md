Matthew Blackwell and Maya Sen's book "Deep Roots" won APSA's 2019 William H. Riker Book Award for the best book on political economy published during the past three calendar years. The book traces how slavery's institutional legacy continues to shape American politics today.

Using a combination of historical data, geographic analysis, and modern survey evidence, Blackwell and Sen demonstrate that Southern counties with higher historical concentrations of slavery exhibit distinctive political attitudes and behaviors that persist into the present—even after controlling for contemporary demographic and economic factors.

The research exemplifies IQSS's approach to social science: combining rigorous quantitative methods with deep substantive knowledge to produce findings that illuminate fundamental questions about how historical institutions shape the present. Both authors are IQSS affiliates who leveraged the institute's resources in their research.
