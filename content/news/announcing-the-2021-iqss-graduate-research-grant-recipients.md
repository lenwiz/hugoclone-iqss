The Institute for Quantitative Social Science is pleased to announce the recipients of the 2021 IQSS Graduate Research Grants, a competitive program that provides crucial financial support for dissertation research by graduate students across Harvard's social science departments. These grants enable students to conduct ambitious research projects that require funding for data collection, fieldwork, specialized software, research assistance, or other resources essential to completing high-quality dissertation work.

The IQSS Graduate Research Grant program reflects the institute's commitment to supporting the next generation of quantitative social scientists by removing financial barriers to innovative research. Recipients represent diverse disciplines and methodological approaches, from experimental and survey research to computational text analysis and spatial studies. The grants provide students with flexibility to pursue their research visions while benefiting from IQSS's broader ecosystem of methodological expertise, consulting services, and collaborative community.

Competition for these grants is rigorous, with proposals evaluated on intellectual merit, methodological sophistication, and potential contribution to social science knowledge. The selection process recognizes that dissertation research often requires resources beyond typical departmental support, particularly for projects employing cutting-edge quantitative methods or collecting original data. Recipients join a community of IQSS-affiliated graduate students who benefit from workshops, seminars, and networking opportunities that enrich their scholarly development. Congratulations to all the 2021 grant recipients as they advance their dissertation research.
