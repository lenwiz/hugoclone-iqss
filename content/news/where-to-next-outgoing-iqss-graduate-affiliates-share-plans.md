IQSS staff piece about departing graduate student affiliates from programs across Harvard and their future plans. Each year, the end of the academic year brings the departure of affiliated graduate students who have been active in social science research.

The departing affiliates head to positions across academia, policy organizations, and the private sector, carrying with them the quantitative training and collaborative research experience they gained at IQSS. Their diverse career paths—from assistant professorships to research positions at think tanks to roles in tech companies—reflect the breadth of opportunities available to quantitative social scientists.

Tracking these transitions serves both to celebrate individual achievements and to demonstrate the value of IQSS affiliation for career development. Many alumni maintain connections with the institute long after departure, returning for seminars, collaborating on research, and mentoring current students.
