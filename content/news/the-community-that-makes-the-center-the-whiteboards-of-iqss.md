**by Gary King**

*IQSS director Gary King reflects on the origins of the writing surfaces that cover the hallways of the Institute, as featured in a [recent article by the Harvard Gazette](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2021/11/take-a-tour-of-harvards-chalkboards-and-whiteboards/).*

When King started IQSS, he interviewed many current and former Harvard deans and presidents to understand what made the best centers succeed. He looked at places like the legendary Cavendish in London, Bell Labs, ISR, and others. Everyone he spoke with had a different story but arrived at the same conclusion. One person would emphasize great space, but then acknowledge that some great centers lacked great space. Others would stress funding, then concede it wasn't really about money. But ultimately, every single person he consulted landed on the same answer: community. As King writes, "It is the intellectual community that makes the center." He had a specific notion of how to build that community at IQSS, described in [an article he wrote about IQSS](https://gking.harvard.edu/files/gking/files/king14.pdf), and notes that "this method wound up working great!"

When IQSS was moving into the new CGIS building, architect Harry Cobb asked King what IQSS wanted on the walls. Other centers displayed great historical photos, but IQSS was a brand-new institute without much past and was "entirely forward looking," so they didn't have old black-and-white photos. King also notes parenthetically that "We're not all men and almost none of us wear suits anyway." Old photos also didn't contribute to community building. So King asked Cobb whether it was possible to install very large whiteboards instead. Cobb investigated and returned saying he knew exactly what was needed — he found an innovative whiteboard wallpaper material that was perfect for the purpose.

The whiteboards have had a tremendous impact on the IQSS community. Rather than people glancing at photos as they pass through, "they instead pour out of their offices and work on real problems together in the hallways." King emphasizes that the hallways on the 3rd floor of CGIS are not merely connectors between spaces but are "integral parts of the building." They help build community, house community, and improve community. King closes with an invitation: "come hang out in the hallways, see the IQSS community in action; we'd love for you to join us."
