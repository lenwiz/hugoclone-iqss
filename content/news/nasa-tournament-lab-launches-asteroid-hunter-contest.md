The NASA Tournament Lab at IQSS challenged competitors to develop better asteroid detection methods, leveraging crowd-sourced innovation to address a critical space safety challenge. The lab, a collaboration among NASA, Harvard Business School, and TopCoder, uses competition-based approaches to solve complex technical problems.

The Asteroid Hunter Contest asked participants to develop algorithms that could identify potentially hazardous asteroids from astronomical observation data more accurately and efficiently than existing methods. By opening the problem to a global community of problem-solvers, the contest aimed to tap expertise beyond traditional astronomy and aerospace engineering.

This initiative exemplified the NASA Tournament Lab's approach at IQSS: using the science of crowdsourcing and open innovation to accelerate solutions to important challenges, while simultaneously generating research data on how distributed problem-solving works.
