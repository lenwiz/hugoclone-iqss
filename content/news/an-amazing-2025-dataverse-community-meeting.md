The IQSS Dataverse Core team recently returned from the 2025 Dataverse Community Meeting, which was hosted by the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. The event represented the culmination of two and a half years of intensive development work, resulting in several major milestones for the Dataverse Project.

## Key Highlights

- A new React-based frontend, expected to be available in production in July 2025
- The launch of *Dataverse Hub* and *Dataverse Marketplace*
- Significant advancements in AI readiness, including full support for the Model Context Protocol (MCP) and Croissant-ML metadata schema. Dataverse was selected as one of the data repositories for the NeurIPS25 conference, described as "the most important AI conference of the year"
- **AutoSage** — an AI Metadata helper built on open source large language models, fully trained at IQSS on the complete Harvard Dataverse data, along with the forthcoming *Dataverse Knowledge Graph*
- A powerful *External Search Tool* that uses natural language queries to interrogate the Dataverse repository alongside traditional keyword and facet-based search
- New integration with high performance computing infrastructures, including the internal FAS-RC cluster, via a tool called **LOOP** that connects OpenOnDemand and Dataverse seamlessly (a collaboration between the Dataverse and HPC teams at IQSS)
- New Large Data Support services deployed at scale

## Community Growth

The Dataverse community continues to expand, with worldwide installations growing from 70 in 2022 to over 135 today. The meeting showcased contributions from institutions and developers across the globe.

The team expressed thanks to the University of North Carolina for hosting, the Dataverse Development and Curation teams for their accomplishments, and the broader Dataverse Community.

## About the Dataverse Project

The Dataverse Project was founded by IQSS Director Gary King in 2006. Its software platform provides preservation and archival infrastructure, enabling researchers to share, maintain control of, and receive recognition for their data through a web browser interface. More information is available at [dataverse.org](https://dataverse.org/).
