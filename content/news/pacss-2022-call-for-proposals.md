The annual Politics and Computational Social Science (PaCSS) conference has issued its call for proposals for the 2022 meeting, inviting researchers to submit work that advances the intersection of political science and computational methods. This conference brings together scholars using innovative computational approaches to study political phenomena, from social media analysis and text mining to machine learning and network science applied to questions of governance, political behavior, and democratic processes.

PaCSS has become an important venue for researchers pushing the boundaries of political science methodology by incorporating large-scale data analysis, natural language processing, computer vision, and other computational techniques. The conference welcomes both methodological innovations and substantive applications, encouraging submissions that demonstrate how computational approaches can generate new insights into political questions or enable analysis at scales previously impossible. Graduate students, postdoctoral researchers, and faculty from political science, computer science, communication, sociology, and related fields are all encouraged to submit proposals.

The conference provides valuable opportunities for intellectual exchange among a diverse community of scholars who share interests in computational approaches to studying politics. In addition to traditional paper presentations, PaCSS typically features poster sessions, software demonstrations, and methodological workshops that foster learning and collaboration. The conference has historically been an important launching pad for innovative research that bridges computational and political science communities. Detailed submission guidelines, deadlines, and information about the 2022 meeting location and dates can be found on the conference website.
