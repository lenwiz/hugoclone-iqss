*by Elizabeth Salazar*

The Institute for Quantitative Social Science (IQSS) launched a program offering Harvard faculty exceptional help in providing extraordinary evidence for their extraordinary claims. Called Extraordinary Claims, Extraordinary Evidence (ECEE), the program aims to catalyze rigorous research that would shift dominant scientific positions significantly.

The program recognizes that many extraordinary claims are "explicitly contrarian positions" that, if validated, reveal flawed assumptions or unsupported conclusions in existing research. Others address subjects not actively studied—topics scholars incorrectly assume are understood or consider unworthy of investigation—yet which hold potential for major shifts in a field's dominant view.

While researchers have strong career incentives to challenge accepted views or explore understudied subjects, greater potential rewards come paired with greater risk of penalty if a researcher fails to defend an extreme position. IQSS created ECEE to support scholars pursuing high-risk research that advances scientific understanding.

## Support Offered

Selected awardees receive supercharged support from IQSS research infrastructure and personnel, including:

- Full-time assistance from a survey research expert or computer programmer
- Dedicated IQSS data science services for research design and data visualization
- Setup assistance in running high performance computing resources
- Access to extensive data curation services and leveraging the Dataverse platform
- Dedicated access to IQSS incubation services

## Eligibility

Awards are open to Harvard ladder faculty (individuals or groups) conducting research with a social science component—defined as "any research focused on human beings holistically, rather than on specific anatomical or physiological systems." Awardees must participate in an extension of the IQSS Peer Pre-Review Program, which accelerates the research and journal review process and helps scholars engage with arm's length, possibly contrarian perspectives on their assumptions and methods.

## Selection Process

An ad hoc committee with relevant field experts reviews the most promising proposals and recommends awardees to IQSS leadership for final selection. A subset of those chosen then meets with IQSS staff to align research needs with available resources.
