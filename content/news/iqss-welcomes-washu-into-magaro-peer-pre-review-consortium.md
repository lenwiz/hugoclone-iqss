*by Liz Salazar*

The Institute for Quantitative Social Science at Harvard University is expanding the Alexander and Diviya Magaro Peer Pre-Review Program—a service designed to improve scholarship by speeding up the journal submission process—into a multi-institutional consortium. The Weidenbaum Center on the Economy, Government, and Public Policy at Washington University in St. Louis is the first affiliate to partner with IQSS, adding its own network of associate editors and peer reviewers to Harvard's existing cohort. Washington University professors Gaetano Antinolfi and Ian Filmore will work with senior preceptor Chase Harrison at IQSS to coordinate the review process for submitted drafts.

Funded by a gift from Alexander and Diviya Magaro, the IQSS Peer Pre-Review Program selects anonymous reviewers to provide direct, thorough feedback—usually within four weeks—on submitted research documents such as grant proposals, pre-analysis plans, or draft manuscripts. The goal is a stronger finalized paper. This process can save researchers one or more rounds of journal submission, rejection, and resubmission, which often takes several months and may not even include constructive comments to guide revision. By refining a paper through peer pre-review, researchers can see publication in a shorter time frame.

Reviewers participating in the program are vetted and matched to research drafts according to their expertise and subject matter. They receive a stipend in exchange for prompt, detailed feedback.

Many Harvard researchers have found the process so valuable that they regularly use PPR when preparing new drafts for journal submission. The program's success has attracted attention from social scientists across the field, and Washington University is anticipated to be the first of many institutions joining the consortium.

"Any scholar knows that journal rejections are routine in academia and can lead to months of delays, if their paper is ever published," said Andrew Reeves, PhD '08, Washington University's director of the Weidenbaum Center and professor of political science in Arts & Sciences. He added that "the peer pre-review process improves efficiency and productivity by giving researchers access to valuable feedback along the way" rather than waiting until a manuscript is complete. "We are excited to offer this invaluable service to our social science colleagues."

The partnership between IQSS and the Weidenbaum Center promises a wider network of reviewers with expertise across more disciplines, strengthening review quality. The program will also expand the number of academic researchers able to use peer pre-review to publish more quickly and advance their research.

"We are thrilled to be partnering with Washington University on the Peer Pre-Review Program," said Gary King, director of IQSS and Albert J. Weatherhead III University Professor. He noted the partnership "will enable both universities to increase the quality of their research" and reduce the time between social scientific discoveries and their application for the public good.

Participation in the Magaro Peer Pre-Review Program is open to anyone at Harvard University or Washington University in St. Louis conducting social science research, broadly defined as any research with a social- or human-based focus.

*Founded in 2005, the Institute for Quantitative Social Science (IQSS) is Harvard University's largest social science research center. IQSS has helped create numerous programs, centers, and initiatives operating within the Institute, across the University, and as independent nonprofits and commercial firms outside Harvard. IQSS is home to programs including the Center for Geographic Analysis, the Human Flourishing Program, and Social Science One, and has launched open-source software and platforms such as Dataverse, OpenScholar, and OpenDP.*
