The Center for Geographic Analysis has unveiled a completely revamped training workshop schedule for 2026, offering Harvard community members an extensive array of geospatial technology and data science courses. All workshops are free for Harvard ID holders and cover essential tools and platforms including ArcGIS, QGIS, Python, R, KNIME, and many other technologies used in geographic analysis and spatial data science.

The 2026 training program reflects CGA's commitment to keeping pace with rapidly evolving geospatial technologies and methodologies. The schedule includes both introductory courses for those new to GIS and advanced workshops for experienced users looking to expand their skills. Topics range from fundamental mapping and spatial analysis techniques to sophisticated applications of machine learning, remote sensing, and big data analytics in geographic contexts. The workshops are designed to serve researchers across all disciplines who work with spatial data in their research.

Registration for the 2026 training schedule is now open, and Harvard community members are encouraged to explore the full schedule and sign up early for workshops of interest. The CGA training program has long been one of the most popular resources offered by the center, providing hands-on instruction from expert staff and opportunities to connect with other researchers working on geospatial projects across the university.
