Professor Intisar Rabb's career as a legal scholar began during a joint program at Yale Law School and Princeton, focusing on comparative issues in Islamic law. At Harvard, she has developed SHARIAsource—a digital platform that makes primary sources of Islamic legal tradition accessible to researchers worldwide.

SHARIAsource represents an ambitious effort to digitize and organize centuries of Islamic legal texts, judicial opinions, and scholarly commentary, creating a searchable database that can support comparative legal research. The project leverages IQSS's data infrastructure and computational resources.

Rabb's work demonstrates how quantitative and computational methods can transform traditionally humanistic fields of inquiry. By making vast textual corpora analyzable through digital tools, projects like SHARIAsource open new avenues for understanding legal traditions, historical patterns, and cross-cultural comparisons.
