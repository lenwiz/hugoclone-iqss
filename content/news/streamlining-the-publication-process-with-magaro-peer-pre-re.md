Stephen Chaudoin laments that "you only get so many bites of the apple with journals"—the traditional publication process is lengthy, uncertain, and offers limited opportunities for constructive feedback before formal review. The Magaro Peer Pre-Review Program addresses this gap.

The program, funded by a generous gift from Alexander and Diviya Magaro, provides researchers with professional-quality peer reviews before they submit to journals. Reviewers read full papers and offer detailed, constructive suggestions, helping authors strengthen their work and avoid common pitfalls that lead to rejection.

By streamlining the path from manuscript to publication, the program aims to accelerate scientific discovery while improving the quality of published research. Since its launch at IQSS, the program has expanded to include Washington University in St. Louis and has been rebranded as rapidPeer.
