The Harvard Dataverse has published a significant new collection from Houghton Library: the Slavery, Abolition, Emancipation, and Freedom (SAEF) Data Collection, making valuable historical research data freely available to scholars worldwide. This collection brings together data related to one of the most consequential periods in American history, documenting the experiences of enslaved people, the abolition movement, emancipation processes, and the struggle for freedom and civil rights in the nineteenth century.

The SAEF Data Collection includes digitized materials, structured datasets, and research outputs derived from Houghton Library's extensive holdings related to slavery and abolition. By publishing this collection through Dataverse, Houghton Library ensures that these valuable research materials are discoverable, citable, and preserved for long-term access. The data supports scholarship across multiple disciplines including history, sociology, African American studies, and digital humanities, enabling both traditional research and computational analyses that can reveal new patterns and insights from historical sources.

This publication exemplifies the growing collaboration between libraries and data repositories to make historical collections more accessible and useful for research. Houghton Library's choice of Dataverse demonstrates confidence in the platform's capabilities for managing diverse digital collections and providing robust infrastructure for long-term preservation. The SAEF collection contributes to broader efforts to document and understand the history of slavery and its ongoing legacies, ensuring that future generations of scholars have access to primary sources and research data essential for this important work.
