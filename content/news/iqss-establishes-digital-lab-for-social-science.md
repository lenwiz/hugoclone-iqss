IQSS has established the Digital Lab for the Social Sciences (DLABSS), a dedicated space and program designed to support computationally intensive social science research and provide researchers with access to expertise, tools, and collaborative environment for digital scholarship. The lab represents IQSS's commitment to advancing methodological innovation by creating infrastructure that enables researchers to work with new data sources, apply cutting-edge analytical methods, and collaborate on projects that push the boundaries of traditional social science.

The Digital Lab provides both physical workspace equipped with high-performance computing resources and a program of support services including consultations, workshops, and collaborative research projects. Researchers working with large-scale text data, social media corpora, digital archives, computational models, or other digital sources can access technical expertise to overcome methodological challenges. The lab fosters a community of practice among digitally-oriented social scientists, creating opportunities for knowledge sharing and interdisciplinary collaboration that often leads to innovative research approaches.

DLABSS fills an important niche in the landscape of research support at Harvard, specifically addressing the needs of social scientists engaged in digital and computational work. Unlike generic computing support, the lab combines technical capabilities with deep understanding of social science research questions and methodologies. This combination enables more effective support for researchers who need to bridge between disciplinary knowledge and computational methods. The establishment of DLABSS reflects IQSS's strategic vision of building comprehensive infrastructure—not just tools and technology, but also human expertise and collaborative spaces—that enables excellent quantitative and computational social science research.
