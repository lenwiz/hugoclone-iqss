Douglas Richardson, Distinguished Researcher at Harvard's Center for Geographic Analysis (CGA), received Lifetime Achievement Honors from the American Association of Geographers. The award recognizes his decades of contributions to geographic information science and its applications.

Richardson has been instrumental in advancing the use of GIS technology in academic research, working to make geospatial tools accessible to scholars across disciplines. His work at CGA has helped establish Harvard as a leader in geographic analysis and spatial data infrastructure.

The lifetime achievement recognition reflects both Richardson's scholarly contributions and his service to the broader geographic community. His efforts have helped bridge the gap between technical GIS development and practical applications in social science, public health, and environmental research.
