The Cooperative Election Study, supported by IQSS, offers key insights into the minds of American voters. Running since 2006, the study surveys tens of thousands of Americans before and after each election, providing unprecedented detail about electoral behavior at the local level.

The study's massive sample size—typically 50,000 or more respondents—enables analysis at the congressional district level, something impossible with standard national polls. This granularity has made the CES an essential resource for researchers studying representation, accountability, and geographic variation in political attitudes.

Hosted on Harvard Dataverse, the CES data has supported hundreds of academic publications and has been used by journalists, campaign professionals, and policymakers seeking to understand the American electorate. The study continues to evolve, adding new questions and methodological innovations each election cycle.
