Researchers at the Center for Geographic Analysis explore drive time estimation's importance in urban planning, transportation, public health, and healthcare accessibility. Their case study examines travel times to healthcare facilities across the United States, revealing geographic disparities in access.

The research addresses a fundamental challenge in geospatial analysis: accurately estimating travel times for millions of origin-destination pairs requires enormous computational resources and sophisticated algorithms that account for road networks, traffic patterns, and geographic barriers.

By developing scalable methods for travel time estimation, the CGA team provides tools that researchers and policymakers can use to identify communities with inadequate access to essential services—information critical for equitable resource allocation and infrastructure investment.
