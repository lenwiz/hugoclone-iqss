IQSS, Pinterest, and Harvard TH Chan School of Public Health scientists collaborated to address the gap in recorded cases of the novel coronavirus in the United States through the How We Feel Project. The initiative launched in early April 2020 as COVID-19 testing remained severely limited.

The project developed a mobile application that collected self-reported health data from users, creating a real-time picture of symptom prevalence across the country that complemented official testing data. This crowd-sourced epidemiological approach leveraged smartphone technology to gather information at a scale and speed that traditional surveillance systems could not match.

The How We Feel Project demonstrated how academic-industry partnerships can rapidly deploy technology to address public health emergencies, embodying IQSS's mission of applying quantitative social science methods to solve real-world problems.
