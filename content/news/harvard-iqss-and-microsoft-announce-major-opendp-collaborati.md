Gary King led IQSS's announcement of a major collaboration with Microsoft to build a platform ensuring data is kept private while remaining useful for research. The partnership aims to develop open-source differential privacy tools accessible to researchers and organizations handling sensitive information.

The collaboration brings together Harvard's theoretical expertise in privacy-preserving computation with Microsoft's engineering resources and practical experience deploying privacy technologies at scale. Together, they aim to create tools that make differential privacy practical for everyday use by researchers and data analysts.

This partnership exemplifies IQSS's approach to building infrastructure that advances social science—recognizing that without robust privacy protections, researchers will increasingly lose access to the data they need to study society's most pressing challenges.
