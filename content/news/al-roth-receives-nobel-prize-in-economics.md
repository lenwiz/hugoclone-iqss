IQSS affiliate Al Roth (Harvard Business School) and Lloyd S. Shapley were awarded the 2012 Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel for their work on the theory of stable allocations and the practice of market design. The prize recognized how their research had moved from abstract theory to practical applications that improve people's lives.

Roth's work on market design has led to better systems for matching kidney donors with patients, assigning students to schools, and placing medical residents in hospitals. His approach combines game theory with careful empirical observation to design mechanisms that produce stable, efficient outcomes in markets where prices alone cannot do the job.

As an IQSS affiliate, Roth exemplifies the institute's mission of advancing quantitative social science that has real-world impact. His Nobel Prize highlighted how rigorous academic research can be translated into practical solutions for significant societal challenges.
