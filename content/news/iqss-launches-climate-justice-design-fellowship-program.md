IQSS has launched the Climate Justice Design Fellowship program, an innovative initiative that brings together data science, design thinking, and climate justice principles to address environmental challenges in ways that center equity and community needs. This fellowship program reflects growing recognition that climate change disproportionately affects marginalized communities and that addressing climate challenges requires not just technical solutions but approaches grounded in justice and participatory design.

The Climate Justice Design Fellowship supports interdisciplinary teams combining expertise in data science, environmental science, design, and social science to develop tools, visualizations, and interventions that help communities understand and respond to climate impacts. Fellows work on projects that might include mapping climate vulnerability in environmental justice communities, creating accessible data visualization tools for community organizations, or developing platforms that amplify community voices in climate policy discussions. The program emphasizes participatory approaches that involve affected communities as partners rather than subjects of research.

The fellowship program positions IQSS at the intersection of data science and social justice, demonstrating how quantitative methods and computational tools can serve values of equity and empowerment. By supporting work that centers justice alongside technical excellence, the program helps ensure that advances in data science contribute to more equitable and sustainable futures. The Climate Justice Design Fellowship has attracted fellows from diverse backgrounds who share commitment to using their skills for social good, creating a community of practice around justice-centered data science and design.
