Early bird registration is now open for the Dataverse Community Meeting 2026, taking place May 12-15 in Barcelona, Spain. This year's meeting is co-organized with the Barcelona Supercomputing Center, bringing together the global Dataverse community in one of Europe's most vibrant and innovative cities. The event promises to be a landmark gathering for researchers, data managers, repository administrators, and developers working with the Dataverse platform.

The Dataverse Community Meeting serves as the premier annual event for the worldwide network of institutions using Dataverse for research data management and sharing. Attendees will have opportunities to learn about the latest developments in the Dataverse software, share experiences and best practices, participate in hands-on technical workshops, and connect with colleagues from around the globe. The meeting typically features presentations on innovative uses of Dataverse, discussions of data curation challenges, and planning sessions for future platform enhancements.

Hosting the meeting in Barcelona alongside the Barcelona Supercomputing Center offers unique opportunities to explore connections between research data management and high-performance computing. The early bird registration period provides discounted rates for those who commit early, and participants are encouraged to register soon to secure their spots. The meeting is an invaluable opportunity for anyone involved in research data management to stay current with developments in the Dataverse ecosystem and to contribute to shaping the future of open research data infrastructure.
