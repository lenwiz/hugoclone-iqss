The Center for Geographic Analysis has undertaken a major geospatial infrastructure enhancement project to address growing demand for robust, scalable systems capable of handling geospatial big data. As researchers across disciplines increasingly work with massive spatial datasets—from high-resolution satellite imagery to detailed geographic information about billions of people and places—the technical infrastructure supporting this work must evolve to keep pace with data volume, velocity, and complexity.

The infrastructure enhancement encompasses multiple dimensions including expanded storage capacity, high-performance computing resources optimized for spatial operations, improved data management systems, and enhanced tools for large-scale geoprocessing. These improvements enable researchers to tackle questions that would be computationally infeasible with traditional GIS infrastructure, such as analyzing decades of daily satellite imagery for the entire planet or modeling complex spatial-temporal phenomena at fine geographic scales. The enhanced infrastructure supports both individual research projects and shared services that benefit the broader Harvard research community.

This investment reflects CGA's strategic vision of maintaining cutting-edge technical capabilities that enable frontier research in geographic analysis. As the volume and diversity of spatial data continue accelerating, infrastructure becomes increasingly critical to translating data availability into research insights. The enhancements position CGA and Harvard researchers to remain at the forefront of geospatial big data analysis, tackling important questions in climate science, urban systems, public health, economic geography, and numerous other domains where spatial analysis at scale generates valuable knowledge.
