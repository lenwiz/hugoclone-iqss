IQSS faculty affiliate Melissa Dell received the American Economic Association's 2020 John Bates Clark Medal, granted annually to an economist under 40 judged to have made the most significant contributions to economic thought and knowledge. The medal is often considered a precursor to the Nobel Prize in Economics.

Dell's research examines how historical institutions—from colonial forced labor systems to authoritarian regimes—shape economic development over centuries. Her innovative use of geographic discontinuities, historical records, and computational methods has produced some of the most compelling evidence for the long-run effects of institutions on economic outcomes.

The Clark Medal recognizes Dell's exceptional combination of deep historical knowledge, methodological innovation, and quantitative rigor—qualities that exemplify IQSS's vision of what social science can achieve when technical sophistication meets substantive ambition.
