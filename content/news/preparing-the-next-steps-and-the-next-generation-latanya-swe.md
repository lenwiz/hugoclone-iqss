Latanya Sweeney's Data Privacy Lab and Public Interest Tech Lab explore digital information risks in today's technology-filled world. The labs serve dual purposes: conducting cutting-edge research on privacy vulnerabilities while training the next generation of scholars who will grapple with these challenges.

Sweeney's approach combines technical investigation—actively probing systems for privacy weaknesses—with policy analysis and public engagement. Her labs have exposed re-identification risks in health data, discriminatory patterns in online advertising, and surveillance capabilities hidden in everyday technologies.

The training mission is equally important: students in her labs learn to think critically about technology's societal implications while developing the technical skills to investigate and address them. Many graduates have gone on to influential positions in academia, government, and industry, carrying forward the public interest technology ethos.
