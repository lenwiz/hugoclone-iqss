Since 2006, Stephen Ansolabehere has been compiling data about who votes in America and why as principal investigator for the Cooperative Congressional Election Study (CCES, now the Cooperative Election Study). Supported by IQSS, the study has become one of the most important resources for understanding American electoral behavior.

The CCES surveys tens of thousands of Americans before and after each election, gathering detailed information about their political attitudes, demographic characteristics, and voting behavior. This massive sample size enables researchers to analyze political dynamics at the state and congressional district level—something impossible with smaller surveys.

The study's data, hosted on Harvard Dataverse, has been used in hundreds of academic publications and has provided key insights into the minds of American voters, including how they evaluate candidates, form policy preferences, and decide whether to participate in elections.
