IQSS announced the arrival of its new Executive Director, Elizabeth Jackson, who comes from Greater Portland Health in Maine and from Harvard's T.H. Chan School of Public Health's Department of Global Health and Population.

Jackson has built a career aligned with IQSS's mission. She most recently served as Chief Administrative Officer of The Greater Portland Health Center and has engaged in health system and community strengthening work with partners across Africa, Asia, and Latin America. She has also led trauma-informed resilience trainings.

Throughout her career, she has developed, implemented, and overseen health and education programs in academic, community, public sector, and health settings. During her time at the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health, she collaborated with partners across sub-Saharan Africa to train and support teams in translating proven research into effective program implementation, while also building capacity-building training networks spanning multiple countries.

Her prior roles include positions at the World Health Organization, the Yale Global Health Leadership Institute, and the Harvard Global Health Institute. Jackson holds a Masters in Public Health from Yale University and a Bachelor's degree from Bates College.

"Elizabeth will formally take her post on July 5, 2022." The announcement expressed enthusiasm about working with her to advance IQSS's mission and its programs and projects.
