Starting March 3, 2025, geographer Dr. Rachel Franklin will join the Center for Geographic Analysis (CGA), part of the Institute for Quantitative Social Science (IQSS) at Harvard University, as Executive Director. Franklin succeeds Dr. Wendy Guan and will lead all operations of Harvard's largest organization focused on geographic research, technology, and teaching, collaborating with Professor S.V. Subramanian, chair of the CGA Faculty Advisory Group. The CGA provides consultation services, technical training, platform development, and sponsored research to support geospatial analysis and GIS projects across the University.

## Research Expertise

Franklin brings over 20 years of experience as an educator and researcher in GIS, spatial analysis, and quantitative methods. Her research centers on the sources and impacts of demographic change at multiple spatial scales, and on novel data and analytical approaches to identify and address that change. Her most recent work explores how smart city technologies contribute to and reinforce socio-economic and spatial inequalities. Past projects have examined regional inequality, population loss, and shrinkage at local and regional scales, while her "Spatial Inequality and the Smart City" project focuses on improving the adoption of smart city technologies.

## Career Background

Franklin launched her professional career in 2002 as a statistician demographer at the US Census Bureau. In 2007, she became Deputy Director of the American Association of Geographers. During that period, she also held lecturer positions at the University of Maryland from 2004–2009, before moving to Brown University in 2010 as Associate Director of Spatial Structures in the Social Sciences (S4) and associated faculty in Population Studies.

## Current Roles

Franklin holds a full professorship in geographical analysis at Newcastle University's Centre for Urban and Regional Development Studies (CURDS) and has headed Newcastle Data since 2022. She joined the Alan Turing Institute in 2021, where she currently serves as Group Leader for Liveability in the Urban Analytics Programme. Additionally, she is a visiting professor at Gran Sasso Science Institute (GSSI) in Italy and a visiting scholar in population studies at Brown University.

## Education

- **BA** in French and Political Science, Indiana University (1994)
- **MA** in West European Studies, Indiana University (1996)
- **PhD** in Geography (minor in spatial analysis and remote sensing), University of Arizona (2004)
