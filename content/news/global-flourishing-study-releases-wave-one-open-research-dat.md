Wave one of the Global Flourishing Study dataset is now available to researchers, representing a partnership among Baylor University, Harvard's Human Flourishing Program, Gallup, and the Center for Open Science. The dataset covers approximately 200,000 participants across 22 countries.

The study measures six domains of flourishing: happiness and life satisfaction, mental and physical health, meaning and purpose, character and virtue, close social relationships, and financial and material stability. This comprehensive approach moves beyond pathology-focused measures to capture what it means to truly thrive.

By making the data openly available, the research team enables scholars worldwide to conduct their own analyses, test hypotheses, and contribute to a growing understanding of human well-being across cultures. The longitudinal design allows researchers to track changes over time as participants are surveyed in subsequent waves.
