During the COVID-19 pandemic, frontline physicians, nurses, and first responders faced impossible choices—who would receive a ventilator, whether to keep working without adequate protection, and how to protect their families while serving the public. In those moments, moral injury, a phenomenon once linked primarily to the military, became an everyday reality in healthcare.

Recognizing this urgent need, the Human Flourishing Program at IQSS, directed by Dr. Tyler VanderWeele, launched a global initiative to advance research and equip leaders across healthcare, military, and first responder sectors with tools for identifying moral injury and promoting healing. Moral injury occurs when individuals in high-stakes roles must navigate wrenching ethical dilemmas where no option feels right, leaving lasting scars on conscience and well-being. The consequences include persistent anxiety, depression, post-traumatic stress, substance use, and an erosion of trust and meaning.

To address the growing prevalence of moral injury, the Human Flourishing Program collaborated with members of the American Psychiatric Association (APA) to integrate "moral problems" into the DSM-5-TR. This historic update, approved by the APA and published in the September 2025 annual update, expands the existing category of "Religious or Spiritual Problem" to "Moral, Religious, or Spiritual Problem." The inclusion acknowledges moral struggles as legitimate conditions warranting clinical attention without pathologizing them and opens new opportunities for research, screening, and intervention.

Dr. Jennifer Wortham, a Human Flourishing Program scholar who led the initiative, stated: "We must address moral injury with the same urgency we apply to physical injuries." Dr. Francis Lu, Professor of Cultural Psychiatry at UC Davis Health System and co-leader of the APA proposal, added that the APA's action acknowledges the urgency of addressing the deep social and moral dimensions of suffering that are often unrecognized. The recognition not only provides a framework to understand moral struggles but also enables charting a roadmap for healing.
