Jeff Blossom of the Center for Geographic Analysis collaborated for six years on a National Geographic Society project—Paul Salopek's Out of Eden Walk, an intercontinental journey retracing the path of human migration out of Africa. Blossom provided GIS expertise to map and document the walk.

The project required creating detailed cartographic representations of diverse terrains across multiple continents, integrating satellite imagery, ground-truth data, and historical geographic information. Blossom's work transformed Salopek's physical journey into a rich digital archive accessible to researchers and the public.

This collaboration exemplifies how CGA's geographic expertise at IQSS can support ambitious interdisciplinary projects that span the boundaries between journalism, science, and the humanities. The maps produced serve both as documentation of one man's remarkable journey and as contributions to our understanding of human geography.
