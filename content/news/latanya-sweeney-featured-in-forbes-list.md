IQSS faculty affiliate and Data Privacy Lab director Latanya Sweeney was profiled in a Forbes list of Incredible Women Advancing A.I. Research. The recognition highlighted her pioneering work at the intersection of artificial intelligence, data privacy, and public policy.

Sweeney's contributions to the field include demonstrating that 87% of the U.S. population could be uniquely identified by just their ZIP code, birth date, and gender—a finding that fundamentally changed how organizations think about data anonymization. Her research continues to expose hidden privacy risks in commercial and government data systems.

The Forbes recognition places Sweeney among an elite group of women researchers shaping the future of AI technology, underscoring both her individual achievements and IQSS's role in fostering groundbreaking research at the boundary of technology and society.
