IQSS has launched Sid, a groundbreaking cloud-agnostic multi-user research computing platform that revolutionizes how researchers access and use high-performance computing resources. Named in honor of Sidney Verba, the pioneering political scientist who championed making research resources accessible to all scholars, Sid provides an intuitive web-based interface that democratizes access to powerful computing capabilities without requiring deep technical expertise in command-line computing or system administration.

Sid represents a fundamental shift in research computing philosophy, moving from the traditional model where researchers must learn complex technical skills to access computing power, to one where sophisticated capabilities are presented through user-friendly interfaces. The platform supports diverse workflows including interactive data analysis, batch computing jobs, visualization, and collaborative work, all accessible through a web browser from anywhere. Its cloud-agnostic architecture means researchers can leverage computing resources from different providers, avoiding vendor lock-in while maintaining consistent workflows.

The launch of Sid reflects IQSS's commitment to reducing barriers between researchers and the computational tools they need for cutting-edge work. By making high-performance computing accessible to scholars regardless of their technical background, Sid enables more researchers to tackle computationally intensive questions and adopt methods that were previously available only to those with specialized computing skills. The platform has become an essential component of research computing infrastructure at Harvard, supporting projects across social sciences and beyond while honoring Sidney Verba's legacy of expanding access to research resources.
