Qualitative research forms the backbone of our knowledge about human society. Social scientists at IQSS explore crime, corruption, race, culture, violence, and well-being using methods that go beyond numbers to capture the richness of human experience.

While IQSS is known for quantitative methods, the institute recognizes that the deepest understanding of social phenomena often requires combining statistical analysis with qualitative approaches—interviews, ethnography, archival research, and case studies. This integration produces insights that neither approach could achieve alone.

Researchers at IQSS leverage the institute's infrastructure to support mixed-methods research, using computational tools to analyze qualitative data at scale while maintaining the interpretive depth that makes qualitative work irreplaceable. This commitment to methodological pluralism strengthens the social science produced within the IQSS community.
