The Dataverse Project is excited to announce a call for proposals for the 2025 Dataverse Community Meeting, which will be hosted at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. This annual gathering is the premier event for the global Dataverse community, and the program committee invites submissions from repository administrators, developers, researchers, data professionals, and anyone working with or interested in the Dataverse platform.

Proposals are welcome across a wide range of topics related to research data management, open science infrastructure, and the Dataverse ecosystem. Potential topics include case studies of Dataverse implementations, technical presentations on new features or integrations, discussions of data curation workflows, explorations of policy and governance challenges, and demonstrations of tools or extensions. Both individual presentations and panel sessions are encouraged, with formats ranging from traditional talks to interactive workshops and hands-on tutorials.

UNC Chapel Hill, home to one of the leading research data services programs in North America, provides an ideal setting for this important community gathering. The meeting will bring together the diverse perspectives and expertise that make the Dataverse community so vibrant and innovative. Whether you're sharing lessons learned from your repository, presenting technical developments, or facilitating discussions about the future of research data infrastructure, the program committee encourages you to submit a proposal. Submission guidelines and deadlines can be found on the Dataverse website.
