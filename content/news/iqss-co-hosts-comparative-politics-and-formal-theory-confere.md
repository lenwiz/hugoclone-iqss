IQSS co-hosted the 8th Comparative Politics and Formal Theory (CPFT) Conference at Harvard, bringing together political scientists who use formal modeling and rigorous empirical methods to study comparative politics. This specialized conference provides a venue for researchers working at the intersection of game theory, formal models, and comparative political analysis to present work, receive feedback, and build connections across institutions. The gathering reflects IQSS's commitment to supporting methodologically sophisticated political science research.

The CPFT Conference has become an important annual event for scholars who apply formal theoretical frameworks to questions in comparative politics, from regime transitions and democratic institutions to conflict, development, and political economy. The conference format typically includes both complete paper presentations and works-in-progress, providing presenters with valuable feedback from expert peers. Graduate students particularly benefit from opportunities to present early-stage research and connect with faculty working in similar areas. The conference fosters a collaborative intellectual environment where formal theoretical rigor and substantive knowledge about politics around the world inform each other.

Harvard's hosting of the 8th CPFT Conference, with IQSS as co-sponsor, brought this important gathering to Cambridge and showcased the strength of formal theory and comparative politics research at Harvard. The event aligned with IQSS's broader mission of supporting quantitative and methodologically innovative social science. By providing both financial support and logistical infrastructure for conferences like CPFT, IQSS helps create spaces where researchers can advance methodological frontiers while addressing substantively important questions about politics, governance, and social phenomena worldwide.
