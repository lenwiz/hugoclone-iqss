**Dear:** IQSS Staff and Affiliates  
**From:** Gary King, Director IQSS  
**Subj:** A welcome back update  
**Date:** 8/19/2021

King opens by noting the IQSS community has been away from CGIS for nearly two years, with exceptions for the Client Support Services team and others. He announces the official return the week of August 23rd.

He describes the pre-pandemic intellectual community at IQSS as unique "not only among Harvard groups, but throughout academia" and outlines goals to preserve the virtual community, reconstitute the physical one, and build on lessons learned about workplace flexibility.

## Our Goals

The mission of IQSS is to "understand and solve society's greatest challenges through cutting edge, collaborative social science." Collaborations span affiliate faculty, institutional partnerships, campus programs, and spin-off programs, nonprofits, and startups.

King emphasizes how informal in-person interactions — at the coffee maker, mailboxes, while waiting to speak with research support teams, during the Dataverse team stand-up in the hallway, or stopping in the Quantina — create opportunities for innovation and partnerships. These interactions "foster the enviable intellectual buzz on CGIS' third floor."

He also acknowledges the benefits of remote work: stepping away briefly for personal needs, finding isolation for focused writing or debugging, and recovering commute time.

The goal is to optimize both: "the vibrant community we all seek" and "the work flexibility we know benefits us individually and collectively."

## What will day-to-day work at CGIS be like?

King asked managers to be as flexible as possible with schedules, provided teams accomplish their goals, maintain informal hallway interactions, keep up with off-site workload, remain responsive, and advance IQSS's mission.

Guidelines include:

- During set work hours, be responsive on email, Slack, phone, or Zoom. Making your schedule visible helps accomplish goals while remote.
- New calendaring and communication solutions are in development. Novel approaches to making the community feel connected are welcomed.
- Keep office doors open when present (unless eating or needing privacy).
- Staff may occasionally be needed on campus on unplanned days, with three days' notice as standard practice for remote workers.
- IQSS hopes to provide laptops, second monitors, and job-related software (pending University approval). Client Support Services will assist with home IT setup.
- New community events, programs, and activities — both in-person and remote — are forthcoming.

## Where Will I Sit?

Staff should initially return to their original shared space, with changes expected. IQSS aims to keep staff and faculty connected and optimize informal collaborations. Full-time on-campus staff will likely retain exclusive space; those working from home will time-share space with others.

Roughly half of staff are estimated to be in on any given day compared to pre-pandemic, so the total staff footprint will shrink by about the same amount. This frees space for shared community areas and for students, researchers, visiting scholars, affiliates, and faculty from across the University. Tea and coffee will be available in the Quantina, though University policies require drinking it in offices or outdoors.

## Public Health Procedures

Eight items summarize University public health procedures:

1. **SAFETY AWARENESS:** Review COVID-19 safety awareness training via the Harvard Training Portal and EH&S website before returning.

2. **VACCINATIONS:** On-campus presence requires COVID-19 vaccination unless a medical or religious exemption is received. Vaccination cards must be uploaded to HUHS, which keeps information private from IQSS leadership.

3. **MASKS:** Required indoors for everyone. May be removed in private offices with doors closed. Fully vaccinated individuals don't need masks outdoors if maintaining six feet of distance. IQSS will purchase approved masks in bulk for the Quantina and main office (K350).

4. **TESTING:** Most IQSS staff with regular on-campus presence will be tested weekly, ideally starting their first day back. A Color account is required for testing, tracing, and results. Testing cadence is visible via Crimson Clear.

5. **HUID and Keys:** Bring Harvard ID and office key. From August 23–30, CGIS access requires swiping ID. After August 31, swipe access is only needed after hours.

6. **EQUIPMENT:** Laptops should travel with staff between home and office. Shared printers should return to the office. Standing desks and chairs must be returned, as partially occupied offices will be shared.

7. **EATING AND DRINKING:** Permitted in private offices with doors closed; in shared offices only if alone and certain no one will enter; outside anytime; and in the CGIS Fisher Family Commons (first floor cafeteria). Quick drinks in shared offices are allowed if masks are promptly re-donned. IQSS petitioned for an outdoor tent behind CGIS but doesn't expect approval by fall semester.

8. **USE OF THE QUANTINA, REFRIGERATOR, AND KITCHEN SINK:** No eating or drinking in the Quantina except brief sips. Gathering without food or drink is permitted with masking. The refrigerator may store meals but must be emptied each Friday. The kitchen sink is only for cleaning dishes/cups/utensils. Coffee and tea remain free but must be consumed in offices or outside. King suggests: "how about inviting someone for a coffee break on a walk around the neighborhood?"

King closes: "I can't wait to see you all. Good luck preparing for the return, and thanks so much for all you've done over the last two years!"

*Gary*
