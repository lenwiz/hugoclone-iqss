Sascha Riaz is a Harvard Government PhD student (G5) and resident affiliate at IQSS. His research examines political economy, with a focus on how historical institutions shape contemporary political and economic outcomes, particularly in South Asian contexts.

As a resident affiliate, Riaz has been an active member of the IQSS community, participating in seminars, collaborating with other researchers, and leveraging the institute's data science resources for his dissertation work. His methodological approach combines quantitative analysis with historical case studies.

Riaz's time at IQSS exemplifies the institute's role in supporting graduate students who push the boundaries of quantitative social science, providing them with the infrastructure and community needed to conduct ambitious research projects.
