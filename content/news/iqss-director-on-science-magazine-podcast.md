IQSS Director Gary King appeared on the Science Magazine podcast to discuss his research on media influence on public discourse and how new data sources and computational methods are transforming understanding of how information spreads and shapes public opinion. The conversation explored King's pioneering work analyzing large-scale social media data to understand patterns of censorship, propaganda, and information manipulation, particularly in authoritarian contexts where traditional research methods face significant barriers.

During the podcast, King discussed how computational social science methods enable researchers to study phenomena that were previously impossible to observe at scale. His research on Chinese social media censorship, for example, revealed unexpected patterns about what types of content governments suppress and why, findings that emerged only through analyzing millions of social media posts. The conversation highlighted both the opportunities and challenges of using massive digital data sources to understand social and political processes.

The Science Magazine podcast provides a prestigious platform for communicating cutting-edge research to scientific audiences and the broader public. King's appearance reflects IQSS's leadership in developing and applying innovative methods to important questions about democracy, information, and governance. The discussion illustrated how quantitative social science can provide empirical insights into pressing contemporary challenges including disinformation, media manipulation, and the role of digital platforms in shaping political discourse. The podcast helps disseminate IQSS research beyond academic audiences, contributing to broader public understanding of these critical issues.
