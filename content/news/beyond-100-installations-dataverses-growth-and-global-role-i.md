The Dataverse Project, developed at IQSS, has surpassed 100 installations worldwide, marking a significant milestone in its mission to make research data sharing accessible to scholars globally. This growth reflects the platform's adoption across universities, research institutions, and government agencies on every continent.

From its origins as the Harvard Dataverse Network, the project has evolved into a truly global infrastructure for research data management. Each installation represents an independent repository where researchers can deposit, share, and preserve their data according to FAIR principles—making it Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable.

The expansion beyond 100 installations demonstrates the success of Dataverse's open-source model, which allows institutions to customize the platform for their specific needs while contributing back to the shared codebase. This collaborative approach has created a network effect where improvements made by one community benefit all others.
