James Robins—IQSS faculty affiliate and Mitchell L. and Robin LaFoley Dong Professor of Epidemiology at the TH Chan School of Public Health—has been recognized by the Committee of Presidents of Statistical Societies (COPSS) with the 2025 Distinguished Achievement Award and Lectureship.

The prestigious annual award, previously known as the RA Fisher Award and Lectureship from 1963–2019, recognizes "outstanding contributions to statistical methods that have had significant impact on scientific investigation."

## What the Award Recognizes

COPSS honored Robins for his role in helping create the modern field of causal inference, for developing groundbreaking methods in that area, for his work on missing data analysis, and for semi- and non-parametric models, as well as the wide adoption of these methods across public health, clinical medicine, and social sciences.

## Career Contributions

Robins is widely recognized as a founding figure in the modern statistical field of causal inference. His landmark 1986 paper introduced some of the most important analytic methods and concepts in the field, including those collectively referred to as "g-methods," which are used for drawing causal inferences from complex observational and randomized studies with time-varying treatments or exposure.

Over a career spanning more than 40 years, Robins has applied his methods to research across epidemiology, public health, and the social sciences, including work on HIV/AIDS treatment, arsenic exposure, and bariatric surgery.

## Reaction

IQSS director and Weatherhead University Professor Gary King commented:

"This is among the least surprising awards in the history of the association. Jamie Robins is a brilliant contributor of novel statistical methods, causal estimation, and innovative thinking."

King added that it is a privilege to interact with Robins and have him as part of the IQSS community.

## Next Steps

James Robins will accept the Distinguished Achievement Award and deliver the COPSS Distinguished Lecture at this year's Joint Statistical Meetings (JSM).
