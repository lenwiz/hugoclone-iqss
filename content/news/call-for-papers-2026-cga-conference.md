The Center for Geographic Analysis has issued a call for paper submissions for its 2026 conference, marking a special milestone as the center celebrates its 20th anniversary. The conference theme—past, present, and future—invites researchers to reflect on two decades of innovation in geographic analysis while exploring emerging frontiers in geospatial science and technology. Submissions are welcome from across all disciplines that use geographic analysis, spatial data, and mapping technologies in their research.

This anniversary conference provides a unique opportunity for the Harvard community and beyond to showcase the evolution of geographic analysis over the past two decades. Researchers are encouraged to submit papers that examine how geospatial methods and technologies have transformed their fields, present cutting-edge current applications, and envision future directions for geographic information science. Topics may span traditional GIS applications, spatial statistics, remote sensing, geovisualization, web mapping, spatial artificial intelligence, and any other area where geographic thinking and tools contribute to research innovation.

The CGA conference has historically served as an important forum for interdisciplinary exchange among researchers working with spatial data and methods. The 2026 gathering promises to be particularly significant as it not only celebrates CGA's contributions over two decades but also charts directions for the future of geographic analysis at Harvard and beyond. Researchers interested in submitting papers should consult the conference website for submission guidelines, deadlines, and information about presentation formats and workshop opportunities.
