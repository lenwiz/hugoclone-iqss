IQSS has announced a new initiative designed to transform academic social science research into market-ready startup ventures. The new industry-academic connections being built will help social scientists increase scholarship impact, harvest data and resources from the commercial world for research, and enable universities and businesses to learn from each other more effectively.

As part of this effort, IQSS welcomes the "Startup Foundry" (with a new name to be chosen soon), which has been moved from its previous home at Harvard Business School. The initiative is led by its director and newest IQSS team member, Debi Kleiman, and is supported by contributors Kelly and Carla Kimball.

## Social Science Startups

While numerous natural science faculty create commercial startups, very few social science faculty have taken advantage of such opportunities. IQSS aims to change this and thereby improve academic social science, advance industry-academic collaboration, and create substantial public good. Social science research, including behavioral science innovations, lies at the heart of some of the largest tech companies and other businesses.

The goal of social science research is to create, preserve, and disseminate knowledge about people and society. In recent decades, quantitative social sciences have shifted from studying phenomena to solving problems, making discoveries, designing public policies, and developing innovative methods that turn qualitative information into quantitative insights.

Industry collaborations can unlock vast datasets, knowledge, and expertise. Both sectors possess complementary resources: "industries and governments now hold the majority of the world's data about the people we study, while the academy develops the pathbreaking methods to analyze them." Establishing this information flow greatly increases the potential for success on both sides.

## Building on Proven Success

The Startup Foundry, in just its first year and a half, has already helped stand up nine new ventures from research labs across Harvard, with five "graduating" to the next stage of their entrepreneurial journey. Examples include:

- One startup received a $3M grant and joined the top startup accelerator program in San Francisco
- Another closed an $800K venture capital round
- A third now has a multi-million dollar pipeline of hospital contracts that will increase maternal health outcomes

IQSS also has a long history of incubating research projects that outgrow their roots. Some have become larger research projects, external nonprofits, centers at IQSS or other parts of Harvard, and about a dozen have become successful companies. IQSS hopes to formalize and build on these efforts in collaboration with the Harvard Office of Technology Development (OTD) and other key stakeholders across the University.
