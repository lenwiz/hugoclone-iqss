In 2015, Latanya Sweeney received a $250,000 MacArthur Foundation grant to engage scholars and students investigating unforeseen consequences of technology. The funding supported the Technology Science Research Collaboration Network, bringing together researchers across disciplines to study how technology affects society.

The network connects scholars who might otherwise work in isolation, facilitating collaborative research on topics including algorithmic bias, surveillance, data privacy, and digital discrimination. This interdisciplinary approach recognizes that technology's social impacts cannot be understood through any single disciplinary lens.

The MacArthur Foundation's investment in this work reflects growing recognition that as technology becomes more pervasive, understanding its societal effects becomes increasingly urgent. Sweeney's network at IQSS provides both the intellectual framework and practical infrastructure for this critical research agenda.
