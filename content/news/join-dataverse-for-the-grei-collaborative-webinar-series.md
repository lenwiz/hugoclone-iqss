The Dataverse Project invites the research data management community to participate in the GREI Collaborative Webinar Series, which focuses on repository resources and best practices specifically tailored for NIH-funded research. This series provides valuable guidance for researchers, repository administrators, and data managers working to meet the National Institutes of Health's data sharing policies and requirements while leveraging Dataverse as a trusted repository platform.

The GREI (Generalist Repository Ecosystem Initiative) Collaborative brings together major research data repositories to provide coordinated support for biomedical researchers navigating data sharing requirements. The webinar series covers practical topics including metadata best practices, preparing datasets for sharing, understanding access controls and restricted data considerations, and ensuring long-term preservation of research data. These sessions are particularly valuable as NIH continues to strengthen its data sharing policies, requiring researchers to plan for and execute robust data management from the beginning of their projects.

Participation in this webinar series benefits both researchers who need to understand how to effectively use repositories for their data sharing plans and repository administrators who support NIH-funded researchers at their institutions. The collaborative approach ensures consistent guidance across the repository ecosystem while highlighting specific capabilities and best practices relevant to Dataverse users. Registration information and the full schedule of topics can be found on the Dataverse website and through GREI Collaborative channels.
