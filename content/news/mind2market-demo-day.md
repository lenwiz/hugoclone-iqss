Harvard researchers gathered for the Mind2Market Demo Day, a showcase event highlighting cutting-edge artificial intelligence projects that have reached the stage where they are ready to transition from academic research to market-ready ventures. The event featured several teams from the IQSS Startup Foundry, Harvard's initiative designed to help social science researchers transform their innovations into commercial applications with real-world impact.

The Demo Day provided researchers with a platform to pitch their AI-powered projects to potential partners, investors, and collaborators interested in bringing academic innovations to market. Teams presented projects spanning various domains of artificial intelligence and social science, demonstrating how research developed within Harvard's academic environment can address practical challenges and create value in the commercial sector. The presentations showcased the maturity of these ventures and their readiness for the next stage of development.

The event represents a growing emphasis at Harvard on bridging the gap between academic research and industry application, particularly in the rapidly evolving field of artificial intelligence. By connecting researchers with partners who can help scale and deploy their innovations, Mind2Market Demo Day exemplifies how universities can accelerate the translation of scientific discoveries into technologies that benefit society while creating new opportunities for research funding and collaboration.
