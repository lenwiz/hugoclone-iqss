<p>The Henry A. Murray Research Archive is pleased to offer the Jeanne Humphrey Block Dissertation Award to a qualified PhD candidate in the social sciences each spring.</p><ul><li>Eligibility - Harvard PhD candidate (3rd year or above) in the social sciences who is not graduating this year</li><li>Amount - $2500</li><li>Deadline - <span><strong>Currently on hold</strong></span> </li><li>Process - Submit a completed IQSS Graduate Student Funding Application via <a href="https://carat.fas.harvard.edu/applicant/newApplication?applicationPurposeId=4&amp;fundingSourceId=7268">CARAT</a>. (Application can be downloaded below.) </li><li>Award Restrictions -  None</li></ul><p><strong>Submit a completed IQSS Graduate Student Funding Application via </strong><a href="https://carat.fas.harvard.edu/applicant/newApplication?applicationPurposeId=4&amp;fundingSourceId=7268"><strong>CARAT.</strong></a><strong> Applicants will need to submit:</strong></p><ul><li>An abstract/description of the project (maximum of 2 pages), which study will be used, and significance of the project to the social sciences</li><li>Letter of recommendation from dissertation advisor</li><li>CV/Resume</li><li>Project budget</li></ul><p><strong>Requirements</strong></p><ul><li>Recipient will archive final data and dissertation on the <a href="https://dataverse.harvard.edu/">Harvard Dataverse Repository</a>.</li><li>Awardee will submit monthly updates on grant progress and confirm when resulting research project has been deposited into the Harvard Dataverse Repository.</li></ul><p><strong>Decision Criteria</strong></p><p>Award decisions will be based on:</p><ul><li>Significance to social science research</li><li>Innovation</li><li>Investigator evaluation</li></ul></div>
