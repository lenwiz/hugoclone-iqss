<p>	The Concourse Computer Lab is located in room K024, CGIS Knafel Building (1737 Cambridge Street). To get to this lab, take the stairs or elevator down one level and go to the left through the open door.</p><p>	The Concourse Computer Lab has sixteen Dell towers with 27" LCDs running Windows 11,  four MacOS-based M1 Mac Studio Max's (2022), two M1 Mac Studio Ultras (2022) with 34-inch widescreen curved monitor, two M2 Mac Studio Ultras (2023) *Currently reserved*, one Mac Pro (2019) with a Pro Display XDR and one Dell Precision 7865 with a 43-inch monitor. The lab has two high-speed, black-and-white laser printers and one color laser printer. </p><p>	The Concourse Lab includes a study table with two Apple Studio Displays (USB-C Only), one LG UltraFine 5k Displays (USB-C Only), nine standard Dell monitors, and one 34-inch Dell Curved Monitor on a standing desk. One of the dell monitors is located right outside of the library for a bit more privacy than our main lab.</p><p>	If you are looking for a place to work but don't need a monitor, we recommend the Fung Library right next to this lab, which also has ample space to work quietly.</p><p>	The Concourse Computer Lab is open 24 hours a day, seven days a week.</p><p>	Special Features:</p><ul> <li>		Lab Help Desk.	</li> <li>		Powerful Mac Pro	</li> <li>		Powerful Dell Precision 7865	</li> <li>		Powerful Mac Studio Ultras	</li> <li>		Six Dell UltraSharp 34-inch Curved Ultrawide Monitors.	</li> <li>		One Pro Display XDR	</li> <li>		Two Apple Studio Displays	</li> <li>		One 43-inch Display	</li> <li>		Three Standing Desks (1 Windows, 1 for macOS, and 1 for Laptops)	</li> <li>		High-Speed Scanner	</li> <li>		Dedicated Work Tables (With laptop stations)	</li> <li>		The Winter Garden relaxation room.	</li></ul></div>
