<div class="event-details__description">
<div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><p><span>Join us for the virtual launch of the Human Flourishing Program at Harvard’s Flourishing Schools Project. This session will introduce a new effort to support school networks in measuring, reflecting on, and strengthening students’ character skills and overall flourishing.</span></p><p><span>The event will also provide an overview of the project’s framework and resources, including both a concise project overview and a more detailed implementation guide.</span></p><p><span>Colleagues across education and research are encouraged to attend and share with others who may find this work relevant.</span></p></div>

<h3 class="hwp-section-heading__title text-medium" id="">
    About the Human Flourishing Program
  </h3>
<div class="hwp-section-heading__description hwp-text-block">
<div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-introduction field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><p>Founded in 2016, the Human Flourishing Program at the Institute for Quantitative Social Science studies and promotes human flourishing and well-being. Its research considers important topics such as family, friendship, virtue, community, work, beauty, forgiveness, religion, purpose, and meaning. The program aims to bring together knowledge across disciplines and attempt to integrate such knowledge into a coherent whole, with the goal of a better understanding of and capacity to promote human well-being.</p></div>
