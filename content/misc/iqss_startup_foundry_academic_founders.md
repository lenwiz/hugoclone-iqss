<h3>Commercialization Help for Academic Founders</h3><p>The IQSS Startup Foundry supports Harvard’s social scientists from any school in exploring the commercialization of their research into a new commercial venture. Our sweet spot is very early-stage concepts born from validated Harvard research. </p><p>We've designed our program for busy faculty members and other Harvard academics who want to see their research make a difference but don't necessarily have the time or business expertise to commercialize it themselves or simply want help doing it. </p><h3>What does the IQSS Startup Foundry Offer?</h3><p dir="ltr"><span>We've built a specialized ecosystem with technical, legal, and entrepreneurial expertise, integrated with offices across Harvard, and extending well beyond the borders of the university. We help bridge academic research and real-world applications, and provide strategic guidance, subject matter expertise, help with university policy, industry and investor connections, business experts to help manage your startup activities, and go-to-market experience specifically tailored for academic innovators.</span></p><p>We focus on key steps in the early-stage startup journey, such as market research, understanding prospective customers, business model development, creating an MVP or pilot projects, and fundraising.</p><p>We connect academics with Harvard's innovation ecosystem, including the <a href="https://innovationlabs.harvard.edu/">Harvard iLab</a>, <a href="https://www.grid.harvard.edu/">the GRID</a>, the <a href="https://otd.harvard.edu/">Office of Technology Development (OTD)</a>, <a href="https://entrepreneurship.hbs.edu/Pages/default.aspx">HBS Rock Center</a>, and Boston-area organizations for programming, community, and non-dilutive funding. </p></div>

<div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><h3>The Faculty Role: Flexible Engagement</h3><p>Faculty participants maintain intellectual leadership (and keep your day job!) while choosing their level of involvement:</p><ul><li>Advisory Role: Provide guidance and expertise while a dedicated Business Driver handles day-to-day development and operating duties</li><li>Partnership Role: Collaborate actively with the Business Driver on strategic decisions</li><li>Leadership Role: Take the helm and drive, more “hands-on” venture creation</li></ul><h3>The Business Driver: Executional and Operational Leadership</h3><p>As needed, some projects will be matched with a Business Driver to help forward the early work to explore commercialization. The Business Driver serves as the project's operational leader. Their role is to:</p><ul><li>Work with faculty to understand their research and its potential applications</li><li>Research relevant industries and market opportunities</li><li>Conduct customer discovery interviews</li><li>Develop a business model with guidance from our mentor network</li><li>Create pitch materials for investors or potential customers</li><li>Handle the legwork in creating the strategies and plans to launch a new venture</li></ul><p>Faculty and academics who explore commercialization find it highly valuable, gaining insights into real-world value creation and often discovering new research directions.</p><h3>How to Apply</h3><p>Projects selected for entry into the IQSS Startup Foundry should have at least one Harvard faculty member with an interest in the startup and be based on their research. Projects are being accepted on a rolling basis.</p><p><strong>If you are interested in applying, please email the </strong><a href="mailto:dkleiman@iq.harvard.edu"><strong>IQSS Startup Foundry Director, Debi Kleiman</strong></a><strong> to begin the process.</strong></p><p>Application Steps:</p><ol><li>Initial consultation with the IQSS Startup Foundry Director</li><li>Complete a simple application and submit supporting materials</li><li>Materials Reviewed by the IQSS Startup Foundry</li><li>The IQSS Startup Foundry selection interviews - conversations with members of our community who can provide more info and ensure fit with program goals</li><li>Notification of admission to the program</li></ol><p>The director of the IQSS Startup Foundry is <a data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="ddf245ec-37d9-4095-bfdb-b00b87753a4e" href="/people/debi-kleiman" title="Debi Kleiman">Debi Kleiman</a>, who is widely known in the early-stage tech startup space. The director of IQSS is <a data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="3326d027-7a21-4fdd-8151-34ba80e1a55f" href="/gary-king" title="Gary King">Gary King</a>; he has started half a dozen successful companies based on technology he developed at Harvard, and advised many others.</p></div>

<button aria-controls="hwp-accordion-1627784619-content" aria-expanded="false" class="hwp-accordion__button js-hwp-accordion__trigger hwp-accordion__button-auto-close" type="button">
<span class="title">
          Who is eligible to apply?
        </span>
<span aria-hidden="true" class="material-icon !hwp-block">expand_more</span>
</button>
</h3>
<div class="hwp-accordion__content hwp-bg-light-base" hidden="" id="hwp-accordion-1627784619-content">
<div class="hwp-text-block">
<p>Any Harvard faculty member in any department or school at the university doing social science research (by which we mean research where the person, group, firm, country, etc., is the unit of analysis). Others associated with faculty can apply if they can recruit a faculty member to be a central member of the team.</p>

<div class="hwp-accordion hwp-bg-light-alternative" data-component-name="accordion">
<h3 class="h4">
<button aria-controls="hwp-accordion-833490786-content" aria-expanded="false" class="hwp-accordion__button js-hwp-accordion__trigger hwp-accordion__button-auto-close" type="button">
<span class="title">
          How do you define "Faculty" or "Academic Founder"?
        </span>
<span aria-hidden="true" class="material-icon !hwp-block">expand_more</span>
</button>
</h3>
<div class="hwp-accordion__content hwp-bg-light-base" hidden="" id="hwp-accordion-833490786-content">
<div class="hwp-text-block">
<p id="faq-1955131-138061320">"Faculty" means any ladder faculty doing social science research at Harvard, at any school, who serve as the intellectual cornerstone of a startup project. These scholars bring groundbreaking research and domain expertise while maintaining their Harvard position. Academic Founders vary in their level of engagement in the startup, depending on their preference. For example, faculty who want deep engagement throughout the venture development process would meet regularly with the team working on the business and advisors, and may continue as advisors post-launch, retaining equity ownership with appropriate university conflict-of-interest disclosures. In rare cases, the academic founder may choose to leave their Harvard position to take an operating role in the new company, but usually they keep their job and continue to advise or participate as a member of the board. Faculty participating in the Foundry program find that they can also extend their research impact in and beyond academia while preserving their position at Harvard, which is incentive compatible with their academic career.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="hwp-accordion hwp-bg-light-alternative" data-component-name="accordion">
<h3 class="h4">
<button aria-controls="hwp-accordion-1222771790-content" aria-expanded="false" class="hwp-accordion__button js-hwp-accordion__trigger hwp-accordion__button-auto-close" type="button">
<span class="title">
          How long is the program?
        </span>
<span aria-hidden="true" class="material-icon !hwp-block">expand_more</span>
</button>
</h3>
<div class="hwp-accordion__content hwp-bg-light-base" hidden="" id="hwp-accordion-1222771790-content">
<div class="hwp-text-block">
<p id="hwp-accordion-1154099348"><span>The program is not time-bound; it is based on milestones. Some go through the milestones faster than others, because of what they are building, where they started, or the nature of their research or the market. We find it often takes 6-18 months for the project to find a business model and market. Exit events (when you will become an alumnus of the program and perhaps co-founder, advisor, or C-suite member of the new company) include milestones such as acceptance into a nationally known accelerator program, raising a pre-seed or seed round of funding, obtaining a contract that funds the company beyond the university, or receiving revenue.</span></p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="hwp-accordion hwp-bg-light-alternative" data-component-name="accordion">
<h3 class="h4">
<button aria-controls="hwp-accordion-2081820540-content" aria-expanded="false" class="hwp-accordion__button js-hwp-accordion__trigger hwp-accordion__button-auto-close" type="button">
<span class="title">
          How does the program work?
        </span>
<span aria-hidden="true" class="material-icon !hwp-block">expand_more</span>
</button>
</h3>
<div class="hwp-accordion__content hwp-bg-light-base" hidden="" id="hwp-accordion-2081820540-content">
<div class="hwp-text-block">
<ul><li><strong>Initial Consultation:</strong> Share your research concept or innovation with our team; we’ll help you refine it.</li><li><strong>Business Driver Identification:</strong> We help find you the right business or operating partner (if needed). Sometimes, we begin with an internal RA or staff member to do preliminary research or an IQSS EIR (Entrepreneur in Residence) before moving to a more full-time professional once the venture has spun out.</li><li><strong>Deep Dive:</strong> You and your Business Driver explore the research's commercial potential. (This is how you might work with a grad student or postdoc: you provide wisdom, they do the homework, and you both learn from the experience.)</li><li><strong>Market Research:</strong> Your Business Driver investigates industry applications and brings findings to you.</li><li><strong>Customer Discovery:</strong> Together or led by your Business Driver, conversations with potential users refine the concept.</li><li><strong>Mentorship &amp; Networking:</strong> Access to our network of industry experts and entrepreneurs.</li><li><strong>Go-to-Market Strategy:</strong> Development of a commercialization pathway, whether through venture funding, customer contracts, or direct market entry.</li></ul>
