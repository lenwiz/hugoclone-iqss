<div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden">Ariel Procaccia is Gordon McKay Professor of Computer Science at Harvard University. He works on a broad and dynamic set of problems related to AI, algorithms, economics, and society. His distinctions include the Social Choice and Welfare Prize (2020), a Guggenheim Fellowship (2018), the IJCAI Computers and Thought Award (2015), and a Sloan Research Fellowship (2015). To make his research accessible to the public, he has founded the not-for-profit websites Spliddit.org and RoboVote.org, and he regularly contributes opinion pieces. </div>
