<div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><p>Matthew F. Wilson, Ph.D., serves as Senior Director of Research Translation and AI Strategy at Baylor University's Institute for Global Human Flourishing. Prior to receiving a Ph.D. in Philosophy from Baylor University, Matthew led a successful corporate career where he held various roles in finance, marketing, and product management. He holds an M.B.A. in Marketing from Indiana University, a B.A. in Economics from Wake Forest University, and an M.A. in the Philosophy of Religion from Trinity Evangelical Divinity School. His book, <em>Ownership Unlocked: 4 Principles to Build Self-Driven Teams That Take Initiative, Innovate, and Win</em>, draws on his corporate leadership experience and doctoral research to present a practical framework for cultivating psychological ownership in teams — the invisible force that motivates people to treat a business like their own. His current research interests lie at the intersection of virtue theory and artificial intelligence, motivated by his belief that philosophy has practical value for human flourishing. Dr. Wilson can be reached at matthewfwilson.com.</p></div>
