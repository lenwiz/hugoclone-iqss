<div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><p>Kevin M. Ryan is Professor of Linguistics at Harvard University, where he served as Department Chair from 2019 to 2025. His research focuses on phonology and poetics, employing quantitative corpus analysis to model the cognitive organization of speech and verbal arts. A central strand of this work investigates prosodic systems (e.g. stress, weight, meter, phrasal phonology), demonstrating how gradient statistical patterns in language data inform phonological theory. He is the author of P<em>rosodic weight: categories and continua</em> (Oxford University Press, 2019).</p></div>
