<div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><p>With a degree in Psychology from the University of Dayton, Phil is happy to be working at <a href="http://j.mp/1FQacy4">an institute dedicated to social science</a> and proud to be part of the <a href="/people/people/data-science-products">team</a> that <a href="https://twitter.com/Harvard/status/635801309053067264">rewrote Dataverse</a> to be more inclusive of all science. It's hard to stop him from jumping up and giving an <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-GUr-cd_OWQ">impromptu talk on Dataverse</a>.</p><p>He's thrilled to see the number of Datavese installations <a href="https://groups.google.com/d/msg/dataverse-community/HceZ-0h3-ys/z706eXxICQAJ">listed</a> at <a href="https://dataverse.org/">dataverse.org</a> grow to more than a hundred, and can often be found <a href="https://groups.google.com/forum/#!activity/dataverse-community/NHqWlf-oAQAJ">answering questions</a> on the <a href="https://groups.google.com/forum/#!forum/dataverse-community">Dataverse mailing list</a>, the <a href="https://chat.dataverse.org/">Dataverse chat room</a>, and the <a href="http://dataverse.org/community-calls">monthly Dataverse community call</a>. Every month, he jots down and sends out the <a href="https://docs.google.com/document/d/1PtUHfBnu1gnjpZ1Uu7sEEWjW9QWAK2S9K62DbPaPHCQ/edit?usp=sharing">Dataverse Community News</a> and always looks forward to the annual <a href="http://dataverse.org/events">Dataverse Community Meeting</a>.</p><p>He gets overly excited about open source (<a href="http://wiki.greptilian.com/talks/2017/javaone-how-to-run-an-open-source-project">and gave a talk about it</a>) and created a dataset in Harvard Dataverse called <a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.7910/DVN/TJCLKP">Open Source at Harvard</a> (below). He co-founded the <a href="http://or8.net/mailman/listinfo/codecraft">codecraft mailing list</a>, has a crazy idea called <a href="https://docs.google.com/document/d/1H-mODHLRcGa0dYpzFuIKRrn558fGNX70XNEybK_oOxM/edit?usp=sharing">Crossroads</a>, and makes silly <a href="http://wiki.greptilian.com/teaching-kids-to-code">mobile games</a> with his kids. He blogs at <a href="http://blogs.harvard.edu/pdurbin">blogs.harvard.edu/pdurbin</a> and his personal website is <a href="http://greptilian.com/">greptilian.com</a>. </p></div>
