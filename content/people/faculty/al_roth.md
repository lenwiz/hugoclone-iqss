<div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><p>	Alvin Elliot Roth is the Craig and Susan McCaw professor of economics at <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanford_University">Stanford University</a> and the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Gund_(philanthropist)">Gund</a> professor of economics and business administration emeritus at <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_University">Harvard University</a>.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alvin_E._Roth#cite_note-AlRothspage-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>	Roth has made significant contributions to the fields of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Game_theory">game theory</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Market_design">market design</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Experimental_economics">experimental economics</a>, and is known for his emphasis on applying economic theory to solutions for "real-world" problems.</p><p>	In 2012, he won the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Memorial_Prize_in_Economic_Sciences">Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences</a> jointly with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lloyd_Shapley">Lloyd Shapley</a> "for the theory of stable allocations and the practice of market design"</p></div>
