<div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><p>Len Wisniewski is Senior Director of Research Computing at <a href="/">IQSS</a>.  Before IQSS, Len was Engineering Manager for the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High-performance_computing">High Performance Computing</a> group at <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun_Microsystems">Sun Microsystems</a>, leading the teams developing the <a href="https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-540-68564-7_1">Sun HPC ClusterTools</a> and <a href="http://docs.oracle.com/cd/E19205-01/819-5264/">Sun Studio Performance Analyzer</a> products.   In that role, he actively supported the <a href="http://www.open-mpi.org/">Open MPI</a> open-source community.   Before entering a management role, Len held software engineering roles at various companies, including <a href="http://www.oracle.com/us/sun/index.htm">Sun/Oracle</a>, <a href="https://www.networkworld.com/article/824134/data-center-f5-networks-to-acquire-acopia-networks.html">Acopia Networks</a> (acquired by F5 Networks), and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thinking_Machines_Corporation">Thinking Machines</a> (acquired by Sun).  His primary technical interests have been in developing algorithms and file systems for parallel and distributed computers.</p><p>Len is a native Philadelphian with a B.A. in mathematics and computer science from the <a href="https://www.lasalle.edu/honors-program/">Honors Program</a> at <a href="https://catalog.lasalle.edu/undergraduate/arts-sciences/mathematics-computer-science/">LaSalle University</a> and a Ph.D. in computer science from <a href="https://web.cs.dartmouth.edu/">Dartmouth College</a>.  Len also has taught computer science and software security practices for the Master of Science in Cybersecurity program at <a href="https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/ms-cybersecurity/master-of-science-cybersecurity-boston/">Northeastern University</a>.</p></div>
