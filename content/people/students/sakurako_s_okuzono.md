<div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><p>	Sakurako is a PhD candidate in the Population Health Sciences Program at the Harvard TH Chan School of Public Health. Her research focuses on the following areas: 1) understanding the social determinants of children's health and well-being, 2) examining the impact of natural disasters, and 3) applying computational methods, including machine learning algorithms, to the measurement of social phenomena. This includes exploring the complex, interconnected nature of composite indices, using remote sensing data to measure neighborhood trends over time, and employing a stochastic intervention framework.</p></div>
