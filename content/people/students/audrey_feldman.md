<div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><p>Audrey Feldman is a PhD student in Public Policy at the Harvard Kennedy School on the Judgement and Decision-Making track. As a research fellow at The People Lab, she supports collaborations with state and local governments to generate rigorous evidence and implement field experiments. She investigates how policy implementation, take-up, and efficacy can be improved using human behavior-informed experimental and economic methods. She is interested in improving trust, transparency, and communication between citizens and their governments.</p><p>Audrey also has research experience in international contexts through previous and ongoing collaborations with USAID and the Notre Dame Poverty Initiative. This work emphasizes the value of investing in early childhood education and development.</p><p>Audrey holds a BA in Economics from the University of Notre Dame. Her research inspiration comes from hands-on volunteer experiences at a refugee resettlement organization in Syracuse, NY, a mentorship and tutoring program at a juvenile detention center in South Bend, IN, and years of fundraising collaboration with small nonprofits.</p></div>
