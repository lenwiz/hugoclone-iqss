<div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><p>			Connie Xu is a PhD candidate in the economics track of the health policy program. Her research interests sit at the intersection of labor and health economics, with a focus on the scientific innovation in the life sciences. During her PhD, her projects have investigated productivity variation in fundamental life-sciences discoveries and the role of scientific equipment in shaping research outcomes. Prior to joining Harvard, Connie worked as a predoctoral fellow at MIT and holds bachelor's degrees in Economics and Statistics from the University of Chicago.</p></div>
