## Workshops Hosted at IQSS in Collaboration with Other Departments

### Applied Statistics Workshop

A weekly, cross-disciplinary tour of Harvard's statistical innovations and applications.

[Learn more about the Applied Statistics Workshop](http://projects.iq.harvard.edu/applied.stats.workshop-gov3009)

### Tech Science Seminars

Weekly series on Technology Science from expert researchers, public interest groups, and others on the social impact of technology.

[Learn more about Tech Science Seminars](https://projects.iq.harvard.edu/techscience/news-events)

### Alberto Alesina Seminar on Political Economy

A seminar series on formal and quantitative political research that integrates the study of economics and politics.

### Working Group in Political Psychology

A semimonthly interdisciplinary forum for psychological and empirical research that examines the microfoundations of citizen and elite behavior.

[Learn more about the Working Group](https://projects.iq.harvard.edu/pol_psych/about-wgpp)

### Research Workshop in Political Economy

Examines how institutions affect political and economic outcomes. (Note: this is a closed workshop.)

[Learn more at WCFIA](https://wcfia.harvard.edu/seminars/political_economy_workshop)

## Additional Trainings and Workshops

### Dataverse

The Dataverse Project team offers weekly assistance to Harvard researchers with uploading, sharing, and finding data using the Harvard Dataverse. Open Office Hours include a Dataverse software demo followed by one-on-one support.

</div>

### Center for Geographic Analysis (CGA)

The CGA provides GIS-related learning opportunities both on campus and online. Beyond credit courses, non-credit options include:

- Self-guided video tutorial series
- GIS Institute (two-week training institute held every semester; research proposal required)
- Cartography Workshop (one day, every SPRING semester)
- GIS for the Humanities and Social Sciences (one day, every FALL semester)
- Basic Introduction to GIS (two-hour workshop, every semester)

[Full details at gis.harvard.edu/training](https://gis.harvard.edu/training)
