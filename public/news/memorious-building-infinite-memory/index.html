Memorious: Building Infinite Memory with AI

September 25, 2025

Home › Research Resources › Memorious: Building Infinite Memory with AI

Forgetting is universal—conversations, names, moments all slip away. But what if it were optional? That’s the ambitious vision behind Memorious, a company founded by Spandan Madan and Gabriel Kreiman at Harvard. Their goal is to reinvent human memory using AI, building tools that allow people to recall experiences, conversations, and information instantly without searching or prompting.

From Research to Reality

The science powering Memorious originates from Gabriel’s lab at Harvard. As a Professor of Neuroscience and AI at Harvard Medical School, Gabriel has spent decades researching how the brain encodes and retrieves memory. His lab’s findings formed the technological foundation for the company. He partnered with Spandan Madan, a Harvard Computer Science PhD with extensive machine learning experience from Microsoft, Adobe, and IIT. Together they recognized the chance to merge neuroscience and AI to create what they term “Ambient Intelligence.”

Memorious builds a “Memorome” for each user—an organized record of all memories drawn from conversations, documents, photos, and location data. The system resurfaces memories contextually and in real time, mimicking the human brain but with perfect recall.

Shaped by Customers

With backing from the IQSS Startup Foundry, the founders dove into customer discovery. Mentors and experienced operators guided them through early-stage company building. They interviewed over 50 potential users, including older adults dealing with memory loss, project managers handling complex information, and AI developers interested in agents with human-like memory. They encountered both urgency and opportunity. Older adults described being “desperate” for help, while professionals saw clear value in tools preserving organizational knowledge.

These insights shaped their consumer iOS app (now in beta) and inspired an enterprise API for memory extraction and retrieval. They’re collaborating with design partners to identify new, memory-native workflows that existing AI models cannot address.

Getting Down to Business

As first-time founders, Spandan and Gabriel had world-class research credentials but limited business training. The Startup Foundry introduced them to commercialization—teaching them to frame their story for investors, design products for real users, and navigate go-to-market strategy. Mentorship from seasoned entrepreneurs helped them refine pitches and build scalable business models.

Building Momentum

Memorious quickly attracted investor confidence. The team closed a $3M pre-seed round led by Mayfield Fund, with additional top Silicon Valley investors. They’re now scaling their engineering team, advancing enterprise partnerships, and moving toward their vision of augmenting human cognition with infinite memory.

Gabriel states: “The IQSS Startup Foundry has played a transformative role in our journey from day zero. We found a family of knowledgeable experts with in-depth expertise whose enthusiasm, camaraderie, and rigor helped us navigate the key challenges.”

The IQSS Startup Foundry offers startup resources, support, and networks to social scientists at any Harvard school interested in commercializing research with an aspiring entrepreneur.
