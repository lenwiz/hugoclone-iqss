From the Directors: A Fall Update

September 05, 2023

Home › Research Resources › From the Directors: A Fall Update

This last year has brought a lot of exciting and transformative advancements at IQSS and across the social sciences. Here are a few highlights.

Dataverse Project

The Dataverse Project continued its community growth worldwide, reaching 100 installations, thousands of dataverses, and millions of dataset downloads. IQSS is undertaking a major restructuring of the Dataverse UI as a “single-page application,” which will enable custom UIs, integration with other applications, and make novel statistical innovations instantly available to users. The Dataverse Project is also part of an effort by the Office of Data Science Strategy (ODSS) at the NIH to launch a new data curation, sharing, and interoperability initiative across repositories. The team is also testing experimental code leveraging large language model (LLM) applications within Dataverse, allowing users to interact with and ask questions about Dataverse conversationally.

Peer Pre-Review Program

IQSS has been expanding the Alexander and Diviya Magaro Peer Pre-Review Program—a service aimed at improving scholarship by accelerating journal submission and acceptance—by building out a multi-institutional consortium. The Weidenbaum Center on the Economy, Government, and Public Policy at Washington University in St. Louis became the first affiliate to partner with IQSS and add its own network of associate editors to the program’s editorial team.

OpenDP

OpenDP, a collaborative project bringing together differential privacy experts and users to produce a trustworthy repository of resources for deploying these technologies, continues to advance. It is becoming the standard body of open-source implementations of “differential privacy” algorithms usable for statistical analysis and machine learning on sensitive data. Under its leadership, differential privacy has been introduced to many companies, the 2020 US Census Data release, and consumer mobile devices.

Research Computing

The research computing team has continued to partner with Faculty of Arts and Science Research Computing (FASRC) to migrate social science users to FASRC. This partnership enables social science researchers to advance complex research through access to leading-edge computing services. The IQSS team has taken main responsibility for building the UI for Harvard research computing services.

Support Services

Technical and administrative teams continue offering key support services to affiliates and the broader IQSS community, including Data Science Services, Geographic Information Systems infrastructure, Sponsored Research Services, Human Resources, and Client Support Services.

Office Space

Following the COVID-19 pandemic, IQSS has used new technology to open up office space for the research community. Affiliates are welcome to book desk space when available.

Looking Ahead

“Our aim is to transform social science research from the art of studying the greatest problems that affect human societies to the science of understanding and solving these problems.” The directors encourage social scientists to share unmet research needs or ideas for scalable solutions, and invite newcomers to join the IQSS community.

Elizabeth Jackson, Executive Director of IQSS

Gary King, Faculty Director of IQSS, Albert J. Weatherhead III University Professor
