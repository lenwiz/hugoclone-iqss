IQSS Computer Labs

State-of-the-art public computing labs in collaborative open-space areas for faculty, scholars, post-docs, students, and staff.

Home › Research Resources › IQSS Computer Labs

Who Can Access?

The labs serve faculty, visiting scholars, post-docs, students, and staff. They use University active directory accounts that need activation. New patrons can sign up via the access request form.

Current/active MIT affiliates who do not have University active directory accounts may sign up for lab access — IQSS will sponsor a University active directory account; the process may take several days for new users.

Lab Spaces

Concourse Computer Lab

State-of-the-art computing labs for faculty, visiting scholars, post-docs, students, and staff.

Training Lab

Dedicated space for instructor-led training events requiring computer access.

K301 Computer Lab

State-of-the-art public computing lab on the 3rd floor of the CGIS Knafel Building.

Government Grad Student Lab

Located in room K003, CGIS Knafel Building. Only accessible by Government Graduate students; requires card access.

Study Tables

Study tables providing an office-like environment, located in the Concourse Computer Lab, room K024.

Helpdesk Assistance

The Lab Helpdesk is here to sign users up for access, offer items for checkout, and provide assistance.

Why Visit?

  • A clean, comfortable, and spacious working environment
  • High speed scanning & printing
  • Powerful computers equipped with a large variety of software
  • Computer Training Lab to host your training event
  • Friendly staff who can help get your questions answered
  • Winter Garden meditation room for taking breaks
  • Open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
